Sandwiches & Delis

Brown Bag Sandwich Co.

Greenwich Village, New York City

Ex-Eleven Madison Park sous chef Antonio Barbieri walked away from fine dining to open NYC's first all-chopped sandwich concept. White tablecloth standards, bodega prices, scratch-made everything.

Dishes from the episode
Chopped Cheese
Beef, onions, peppers and American cheese all chopped and worked together on a flat-top griddle, then stuffed into a hero roll. NYC bodega culture executed with Michelin-level precision.
Italian Combo
Prosciutto, soppressata, sharp provolone, fresh mozzarella, red onion, tomato, pickled cherry peppers and Italian dressing on a toasted hero. House-cured meats set it apart.
Spicy Chicken Cutlet
Fine-dining-quality breaded chicken breast with house hot sauce and coleslaw. The classic sandwich format, executed at a level the competition can't match.
Hot Roast Beef & Cheddar
Chef's personal favourite — slow-cooked roast beef, sharp cheddar, simple and deeply savoury. The restraint is the point.
Chicken Caesar Wrap
Romaine, parmesan, house Caesar dressing and shredded chicken in a toasted flour tortilla. Made-from-scratch Caesar lifts the everyday wrap into something worth coming back for.
Summer Menu Take

Fine dining technique applied to casual sandwiches is a premium summer lunch offer that prints money. House-cured meats and scratch sauces are what separate this from everywhere else. A chopped Italian, a hot chicken cutlet, and a roast beef — three sandwiches done brilliantly is better than eight done averagely.

Salvo's

Ridgewood, New York City

Sal started making sandwiches in his home kitchen and delivering them himself. Now making 100+ a day from a brick-and-mortar shop. Italian sandwiches and sides built on quality bread, bold flavours, and zero compromise on ingredients.

Dishes from the episode
Mortadella, Burrata & Pistachio Pesto
Thick-cut mortadella layered with fresh burrata and a vivid pistachio pesto brightened with lots of lemon zest. Simple, stunning, immediately seasonal.
Eggplant Parm
Rustic fried aubergine, sauced, baked, dripping with good olive oil. No shortcuts, proper crust on the aubergine, generous with everything.
Sausage, Potato, Leek & Gorgonzola Cream
Italian pork sausage with soft potatoes, sweet leeks and a funky gorgonzola cream layered onto Otway Bakery bread. A sandwich that tastes like a whole meal.
Roast Beef Genovese
Slow-cooked beef with the sweet, intensely caramelised depth of Genovese onion ragù, with braised carrot. Based on the Neapolitan Sunday sauce tradition.
Lasagna
Beautifully delicate house pasta sheets, enough crispness on top to keep it interesting, finished with a fruity drizzle of good olive oil.
Caponata
Sicilian sweet and sour aubergine relish with tomato, celery, capers and olives. A brilliant simple side that works hot or cold.
Summer Menu Take

Mortadella + fresh burrata + pistachio pesto with lemon zest is a summer sandwich or sharing plate that writes itself. The caponata is also a perfect summer side — cold, vibrant, aubergine at its best in July. Build a summer Italian antipasto board around these flavours.

Diners & American

Kellogg's Diner

Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Est. 1928

A 100-year-old Williamsburg diner, 24 hours, seven days a week — revived by new owners who brought in Chef Jackie Carnesi to inject her Texas roots. Diner classics with a Tex-Mex twist that feels natural, not gimmicky.

Dishes from the episode
Guajillo Braised Short Rib Hash
Slow-braised short rib shredded and crisped on the flat-top with potatoes and guajillo chilli sauce. Served with eggs. The best breakfast hash you'll find in New York.
Chilaquiles Verdes
Fried tortilla chips softened in bright tomatillo salsa, topped with crema, cotija cheese and fried eggs. Textures all the way through, bold and acidic.
Enchiladas Suizas
Corn tortillas stuffed with chicken in creamy tomatillo-green sauce, gratineed with Swiss cheese. A diner doing something more interesting than it needs to.
Chicken Fried Steak
Tenderised steak pressed in seasoned flour and fried until shattering crispy, drenched in white pepper gravy. Texas diner in one plate.
Patty Melt
Beef patty, slow-griddled onions and Swiss cheese melted between two slices of rye bread. Pressed on the flat-top until bronzed. A diner original that deserves to be on every menu.
Cheesy Shrimp & Grits
Stone-ground grits cooked in cream and cheddar, topped with seasoned shrimp in a butter sauce. Southern American comfort done right.
Cornmeal Masa Pancakes
Gluten-free pancakes made with corn masa flour — buttery, slightly sweet, with a subtly savoury note from the masa. Served with maple syrup and butter.
Summer Menu Take

Taking a diner classic and giving it one unexpected twist — here Tex-Mex — is the whole model. Guajillo braised short rib hash is a brilliant summer brunch dish: make the braise, crisp it on the flat-top, serve with eggs. The patty melt is also a summer menu staple that punches above a standard burger.

Gage & Tollner

Downtown Brooklyn — Est. 1879

A 143-year-old Brooklyn institution — seafood and steaks since 1879, revived to its original gaslit glory. Pastry Chef Caroline Schiff anchors the kitchen with a serious American baking programme that's the heart of the restaurant.

Dishes from the episode
House Sourdough Bread
Long-fermented house sourdough with a proper open crumb and dark crust. The bread programme as a statement of intent — it tells guests the kitchen takes everything seriously.
Baked Alaska
Sponge base, ice cream centre, dramatically torched Swiss meringue exterior. A retro showpiece dessert that turns every head in the room when it arrives. Theatre and deliciousness in one dish.
New York Cheesecake
Dense, creamy, impossibly smooth — the classic New York slice in its purest form. Graham cracker base, no frills, nothing to hide behind. Seasonal fruit compote on the side.
Classic American Seasonal Pastries
A rotating pastry programme built on American baking heritage — pies, layer cakes, custards — with seasonal fruit driving the specials. Summer: peach pie, berry buckle, strawberry layer cake.
Summer Menu Take

The Baked Alaska as a summer showpiece dessert — retro, theatrical, unmissable. A plate arriving at a table on fire turns every head in the room and drives orders. Revive a classic American dessert with a seasonal ice cream flavour inside: summer berry, salted caramel, lemon curd. It photographs itself.

Hamburger America

SoHo, New York City

George Motz — burger scholar, documentary filmmaker, obsessive — opened a luncheonette-style restaurant as a monument to the American hamburger. Named top 100 NYC restaurants by the New York Times in its first year. 11 stools, one focus.

Dishes from the episode
Oklahoma Fried Onion Burger
Beef ball smashed hard into a pile of shredded onions directly on the flat-top — the onions fry into the meat, caramelise, and become inseparable from the patty. American cheese, soft white bun. One of the most copied burgers in the world right now.
Classic Smash Burger
Double thin patty, American cheese, minimal toppings. All about the crust from the flat-top and the fat in the beef. The restraint is the craft.
The Chester (Patty Melt)
Burger patty on rye bread with griddled onions and melted Swiss cheese, pressed on the flat-top until bronzed. Motz's personal variation on a diner classic.
Monthly Regional American Burger
A rotating "heritage burger" — one different historic American style each month (steamed Connecticut cheeseburger, Oklahoma onion, Jucy Lucy, slugburger). Drives repeat visits and social media content effortlessly.
NYC Egg Cream
Chocolate syrup, soda water, cold milk — shaken into a frothy New York soda fountain classic. No eggs, despite the name. The perfect cold drink to pair with a burger in summer.
Summer Menu Take

The Oklahoma Fried Onion Burger is achievable on any flat-top and is having a major moment. A monthly "heritage burger special" that rotates is a compelling summer mechanic — it gives regulars a reason to return, drives social content without effort, and tells a story of craft and history. One changing special, done perfectly.

Pizza & Italian

Una Pizza Napoletana

Lower East Side, New York City

Anthony Mangieri has been named #1 pizzeria in the world three times. Every ball of dough is mixed, shaped and baked by one person. Five pizzas on the menu. Naturally-leavened, wood-fired, made daily. The single-minded pursuit of perfection, applied to pizza.

Dishes from the episode
Marinara
San Marzano tomato, garlic, dried oregano, extra virgin olive oil. No cheese. Four ingredients, maximum confidence. The oldest pizza, the truest test of the dough.
Margherita
San Marzano tomato, fior di latte mozzarella, fresh basil, olive oil. Spare and perfect. The benchmark against which every other pizza is measured.
Cosacca
Tomato, aged pecorino romano, fresh basil — no mozzarella. Salty, sharp, ancient. The pizza that predates the Margherita.
Long-Fermented Naturally-Leavened Dough
48–72 hour fermentation, hydration and proof time adjusted daily for weather and humidity. The dough is why the pizza is #1. Light, airy, complex flavour, char that doesn't taste burnt.
Made-Daily Sorbetto & Ice Cream
Small seasonal selection made fresh each day. Served to balance the richness after pizza. Simple dessert that rounds the meal with zero fuss.
Summer Menu Take

The Marinara — four ingredients, stripped back, utterly confident — is the most underrated pizza on earth. For summer: strip the menu back, simplify, make fewer things better. A longer-fermented dough for summer (warmer kitchen = faster proof, so adjust) produces a lighter, more digestible pizza that suits the season. And a daily-made sorbet as dessert is the simplest, most elegant summer closer you can offer.

Pizza & Italian

Antico Nuovo

Los Angeles, CA

Chef Arturo Rojas draws on centuries-old Italian preservation and fermentation techniques. Ancient meets modern — slow-braised offal stuffed into handmade pasta, wood-fired focaccia, house-churned ice cream. Italian cooking as archaeology.

Dishes from the episode
Plin Dell'Alta Langa
Tiny pillow-shaped agnolotti stuffed with braised beef cheek, pancetta, and rabbit — pinched by hand, finished in brown butter and sage. Complex, ancient, deeply savoury. The most labour-intensive pasta on the menu and worth every second.
Beef Cheek & Veal Tongue Ragù
Slow-braised secondary cuts broken down into a rich, dark ragù tossed through house pasta. Offal cooking done without apology — low-cost ingredients, extraordinary flavour.
Wood-Fired Focaccia with Duck Pâté
Thick-crust focaccia pulled from the wood oven, topped with silky house duck liver pâté. Hot bread, cool pâté, immediate satisfaction.
Wood-Fired Focaccia with Burrata
The same focaccia, stripped back: fresh burrata, olive oil, sea salt. The bread is so good it doesn't need anything more.
House-Churned Pistachio Ice Cream
Made fresh in-house, served as the dessert. Intensely green, nutty, not too sweet. The kind of ice cream that makes guests reconsider everything.
Summer Menu Take

Hot focaccia from the oven with fresh burrata and olive oil is one of the best summer sharing starters you can serve — quick, dramatic, aromatic, and guests love tearing into it. The duck pâté focaccia is also a brilliant summer starter that requires almost no effort at service. And if you're making ice cream in-house, pistachio in summer is a crowd-stopper.

Manteca

Shoreditch, London

Chef Chris Leach and partner David Carter built Manteca around one rule: whole animal only. Every pig, lamb, and duck arrives as a carcass. Pasta is the restaurant's heart — the off-menu pig skin ragù has a cult following that calls ahead to reserve it.

Dishes from the episode
Pig Skin Ragù with Parmesan
Slow-braised pig skin broken down into a silky, gelatinous ragù tossed through pasta, finished with aged parmesan. So popular it's become an off-menu item — regulars call ahead to reserve it.
Brown Crab Cacio e Pepe
Fresh brown crab meat folded through cacio e pepe pasta. The brininess of the crab replaces the usual salty cheese hit, making it lighter and more coastal. Summer in a bowl.
'Nduja-Steamed Mussels with Cream, Parsley & Sourdough
Live mussels steamed open in a sauce of nduja-spiked cream and parsley. Spicy, porky, oceanic. Served with thick sourdough to mop the pot. The perfect pub dish.
Duck Liver Parfait with Black Date Jam & Grilled Bread
Impossibly smooth duck liver parfait, sweet-dark date jam for contrast, thick slices of bread charred on the grill. One of the best three-component starters in London.
Pig Head Fritti with Pickled Celery
Crispy-fried pressed pig head, served with sharp pickled celery to cut through the richness. Whole-animal cooking that tastes like an indulgence.
House Stracciatella with Olive Oil
Silky fresh stracciatella made in-house, dressed with just good olive oil and flaked sea salt. The kind of dish that only works with exceptional dairy.
Summer Menu Take

'Nduja-steamed mussels with cream, parsley and sourdough might be the perfect summer pub dish — quick to cook, dramatic to serve, shareable, works with a pint or a cold white wine. The duck liver parfait with date jam and grilled bread is equally strong: three components, make-ahead, plated in 30 seconds, genuinely impressive. Both should be on your summer menu.

Fire, Seasonal & French

Dunsmoor

Eagle Rock, Los Angeles

Chef Brian Dunsmoor cooks everything over live fire — no electricity in the kitchen. No blenders, no processors, no stand mixers. Two hearths and a wood oven do everything. Southern-inspired American cooking that celebrates what fire does better than any machine.

Dishes from the episode
Wood-Roasted Oysters with Bottarga Butter
Oysters placed directly on the fire until they pop open, then finished with bottarga-spiked compound butter that melts into the shell. Umami bomb, done in three minutes.
Edna's Sour-Milk Cornbread
Baked in cast iron in the wood oven with sour milk (same principle as buttermilk), served still warm with cultured butter and honey. Named after a family recipe. The crust crackles; the inside steams.
Grilled Quail with Pepper Jelly
Spatchcocked quail over coals until charred and juicy, served with house pepper jelly — sweet, spicy, sticky. A Southern American classic, brilliant on a summer grill menu.
Pork & Green Chilli Stew
Pork shoulder slow-cooked in a green chilli broth by the coals over many hours. Deep, smoky, warming — the kind of dish that only improves the longer it sits by the fire.
Mushroom-Crusted Pork Chop
Thick pork chop rolled in dried mushroom powder to form a dark, savoury crust, then grilled over the live fire. The mushroom crust deepens with the heat and tastes like concentrated umami.
Braised Greens (Cooked by the Coals)
Dark leafy greens slow-cooked next to the coals until sweet, silky, and deeply smoky. The simplest side dish, made extraordinary by the fire.
Summer Menu Take

Wood-roasted oysters with compound butter are the perfect summer starter — dramatic, fast, and the flavour is impossible to achieve any other way. The bottarga butter can become any compound butter: nduja, herb, smoked paprika. Get a bag of live oysters, put them on the grill, spoon in butter when they open. That's your summer special sorted. The mushroom-crusted pork chop is also a technique worth stealing.

Fire, Seasonal & French

Le Chêne

West Village, New York City

Chef Alexia Duchêne — 29 years old, from Paris — opened Le Chêne with her husband in the West Village. French technique applied with a New York twist. Technically refined but never precious. The most exciting French restaurant in New York.

Dishes from the episode
Crab Thermidor
The whole crab shell stuffed with picked crab meat in a vadouvan-spiced béchamel, gratineed with cheese until bubbling. A French classic reinvented — the vadouvan (a French-Indiancurry spice) brings warmth and depth without overwhelming the crab.
Pork, Potato & Eel Pithivier
Slow-cooked pork, potato and smoked eel sealed in puff pastry and baked until golden, served with a beetroot condiment. A showstopping pastry parcel with a great reveal when cut open at the table.
Foie Gras Terrine with Artichoke
Smooth, cold foie gras terrine with artichoke to cut through the richness. A classic French charcuterie plate — elegant and unfussy.
Uni French Toast with Bone Marrow & Pepper Cream
Brioche soaked and griddled until custardy, topped with sea urchin and bone marrow with a pepper cream. The ultimate indulgent umami plate — ocean meets land in one bite.
Halibut & Scallop Quenelle
A hand-shaped quenelle of poached fish — an ancient French preparation — delicate, precise, technically demanding. The technique is 200 years old and still perfect.
Summer Menu Take

A pithivier as a summer sharing starter — puff pastry parcel filled with a seasonal filling (crab and leek, slow-roasted lamb, mushroom and tarragon) — is a showstopping dish that photographs brilliantly, travels well through service, and has a great reveal when cut. The crab thermidor with vadouvan is also a brilliant summer special: familiar format, unexpected spice, peak crab season.
