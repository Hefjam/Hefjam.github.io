Brunch

7 ideas Mon–Sun mornings — pushing the brunch format beyond avocado toast, towards thoughtful, repeatable crowd-pleasers with one show-stopping boundary push
TopJaw Brunch
Boundary Push Elevated

Hide, Mayfair

Piccadilly, London — Michelin Star

Ollie Dabbous's nature-led fine dining restaurant above a bar. The ground-floor brunch is the most achievable entry-point into Hide's philosophy — dishes built around great British produce, treated with exceptional technique but served casually.

Key Dishes
Lobster Benedict
Whole lobster claw and tail, poached egg, house Hollandaise on a brioche muffin. The Hollandaise is made with lobster butter for depth. The ultimate Benedict upgrade.
Smoked Eel Omelette
Silky, barely-set omelette folded over house-smoked eel, crème fraîche, chive. British fish, French technique — the combination that woke up a generation of brunch chefs.
Birch Sap & Honey Croissants
Laminated dough enriched with foraged birch sap instead of water. Served with cultured butter and seasonal honey. The narrative is the sell — where did this ingredient come from?
Acai Bowl with House Granola
Scratch-made granola with British honey and spelt, house-blended acai. The non-savoury option done properly — not a smoothie-bar afterthought.
Menu Application

The Lobster Benedict framework (upgrade the protein, upgrade the base, upgrade the sauce) is the one plate that positions you above every brunch spot in town. Even a braised beef cheek Benedict on a house-toasted sourdough muffin with truffle Hollandaise signals "we mean it." Birch sap is too niche — but a foraged honey croissant with cultured butter is entirely achievable and giftable.

Fallow, St James's

St James's, London — Michelin Bib Gourmand

Jack Croft and Will Murray's waste-first restaurant where every menu item has a "why this exists" story. Their brunch menu is the most-copied by ambitious UK restaurants right now — mixing zero-waste philosophy with dishes that are genuinely fun to eat.

Key Dishes
Black Pudding Benedict on Honey Waffle
Crispy blood sausage, poached egg and Hollandaise on a house-made honey waffle instead of an English muffin. The waffle is the genius move — sweet-savoury contrast, great texture, and it travels the dish into "you won't find this anywhere else" territory.
Hash Browns with Walnut Ketchup
Shredded and pressed potato cakes, perfectly crisp, served with a house walnut ketchup (fermented walnuts, vinegar, spice). Replaces tomato ketchup with something far more interesting.
Mushroom Royale
A croissant bun (baked in-house, pressed before service) filled with a rich mushroom fricassée, topped with a poached egg and mushroom hollandaise. All-day umami satisfaction.
Corn Ribs with Beef Butter
Corn cobs cut into rib-shaped strips, deep-fried until they curl, tossed in beef-fat butter with chilli. Uses whole cob — an Instagram-ready, snackable brunch side.
Chelsea Tart (from leftover whey)
Classic Chelsea tart dough enriched with the whey left over from making butter. Spiced currants, brown butter glaze. A pastry that tells a story on every menu it appears on.
Menu Application

The honey waffle base for a Benedict is immediately actionable — make it in-house with good local honey and run it as your house Benedict base all season. The corn ribs are a perfect brunch side: cheap, dramatic, addictive. The walnut ketchup could become your house condiment — one fermented sauce that signals the whole ethos. Target one dish from this card, execute it perfectly, own it.

Imad's Syrian Kitchen

Soho, London

Imad Alarnab's Syrian kitchen in Soho, built from zero after Imad fled Damascus. The brunch menu applies Syrian flavour vocabulary to Western brunch formats — the result is the most interesting Benedict riff in London right now.

Key Dishes
Shawarma Benedict
Za'atar-spiced crumpet as the base, spiced rotisserie chicken thigh, poached egg, house Hollandaise spiked with sumac. The cultural translation of the Benedict formula is flawless — everyone recognises the architecture, but nothing tastes expected.
Ful Medames
Slow-cooked fava beans with olive oil, garlic, cumin and lemon. Topped with a poached egg and crunchy fried onions. Levantine breakfast staple — hearty, healthy, vegan-by-default.
Knafeh French Toast
Thick brioche soaked in orange blossom custard, fried until crunchy, topped with warm cheese and shredded kadaifi pastry, drizzled with rose syrup. Syrian dessert meets French toast — works brilliantly as a brunch showpiece.
Fattoush with Seasonal British Veg
Toasted sourdough croutons, heritage tomatoes, cucumber, radish, purslane, sumac-spiked dressing. Adapted to use whatever's local. A dish that doesn't belong in a brunch context until it appears on your table and makes total sense.
Menu Application

The za'atar crumpet base is the single most transferable idea here — make your own crumpets or buy quality ones, toast in za'atar butter, and use as the house Benedict base for your "boundary push" weekly special. The ful medames is a legitimately great, cheap, vegan main that lifts your plant-based offer overnight. Pick the Shawarma Benedict as a weekend special and watch the social media do the work for you.

Roe, Canary Wharf

Canary Wharf, London — Fallow Sister

The Fallow team's waterside restaurant, focused on fish, seafood and produce-led dishes. Their brunch is built around the same waste-first thinking as Fallow but leans into British coastal produce. The pressed croissant sandwich format is the move everyone's stealing.

Key Dishes
Croissant Royale — Crispy Portobello
A croissant pressed flat (like a panini), filled with whole crispy portobello mushroom, melted taleggio, truffle aioli and pickled shallot. The pressing collapses the layers into something crisp on the outside, molten and layered within.
Croissant Royale — Salt Cod & Green Sauce
Salt cod, crispy potatoes, house verde sauce (parsley, capers, anchovy, lemon) pressed in a croissant. A brunch that tastes like a Spanish coastal holiday.
Cured Salmon, Crème Fraîche & Pickled Cucumber
House 48-hour dry-cured salmon on sourdough rye, house crème fraîche, shaved pickled cucumber and dill oil. Clean, refreshing, premium.
Menu Application

The pressed croissant format is immediately achievable with any panini press and a quality all-butter croissant. Portobello mushroom + taleggio + truffle aioli as a filling is a lunch and brunch item that works year-round. Upgrade "toasted sandwich" to "pressed croissant" and you shift the entire perception of your light-lunch offer at near zero extra cost.

Daisy Green Collective

Multiple London Sites — Australia-Influenced

London's leading Australian-influenced brunch group. The format that redefined British brunch culture: fresh, seasonal, vegetable-forward, uncompromising on ingredients, excellent coffee. Everything aspirational brunch now traces back to this approach.

Key Dishes
Sweetcorn Fritters with Marinated Feta
Whole corn kernels folded into a light batter, fried into crispy-edged cakes, served with marinated feta, poached eggs, roast cherry tomatoes and chipotle mayo. The dish that launched ten thousand brunch menus and still works.
Smashed Avo with Sumac & Pomegranate
Crushed avocado on house sourdough with sumac, pomegranate seeds, preserved lemon, chilli flakes and optional poached eggs. The extra seasoning is what makes it different.
Shakshuka with House-Baked Flatbread
Eggs poached in spiced roasted pepper and tomato sauce, with feta and fresh herbs, served with a warm house flatbread for scooping. A guaranteed crowd-pleaser across all demographics.
House-Made Granola with Seasonal Fruit
Buckwheat, oat and seed granola, toasted in local honey, served with Greek yoghurt and whatever's in season. The lighter brunch option that doesn't feel like a compromise.
Menu Application

Sweetcorn fritters are peak summer brunch: August sweetcorn is extraordinary right now, and the dish costs almost nothing to produce at scale. For a market town crowd, shakshuka is accessible and comforting — but use your own flatbread (even a par-baked tear-and-share) and it's a completely different proposition to the versions people have at home. These are your volume workhorses; the other cards are your edge.

Kellogg's Diner

Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Est. 1928

A 100-year-old Williamsburg diner revived with Texas roots by chef Jackie Carnesi. Open 24/7 — the ultimate test of a brunch menu that works. Classics executed with fine-dining precision; nothing is an afterthought. The diner format distilled to its highest form.

Key Dishes
Guajillo Braised Short Rib Hash
Short rib slow-braised in guajillo chilli, shredded and crisped on a flat-top with diced potato and onion. Served with eggs your way. The best breakfast hash in New York — braising liquor deglazes the whole pan.
Chilaquiles Verdes
Fried tortilla chips softened just enough in bright tomatillo salsa verde, topped with crema, cotija and fried eggs. Acidic, spicy, textural — the Tex-Mex brunch dish that's had its moment in London and still lands every time.
Patty Melt on Rye
Beef patty, slow-griddled white onions and Swiss cheese pressed between marble rye and cooked on the flat-top until bronzed. Not a burger. Better than a burger.
Cornmeal Masa Pancakes
Gluten-free pancakes made with masa harina — buttery with a subtle savoury note from the corn. Served with maple and whipped butter. A pancake that tastes different to every other pancake.
Menu Application

The braised short rib hash is the perfect brunch main: you're already slow-cooking for mains — use the offcuts and braising liquor for Saturday morning service. The cornmeal pancake is a subtle swap that gives your pancake stack a USP and a story. The patty melt deserves to be on every British pub brunch menu — it isn't yet. That's your gap.

The Benedict Spectrum

Strategy Framework · Not a Restaurant

The Eggs Benedict is the most culturally elastic brunch format — it can be a crowd-pleaser or a boundary push depending solely on what you put on top and underneath. The London brunch scene in 2025 is defined by creative Benedict riffs. Here's the full spectrum from accessible to adventurous.

The Spectrum — Low to High Boundary Push
Classic — English muffin, back bacon or ham, Hollandaise
The baseline. Everyone knows it, everyone orders it. Works everywhere. Your volume dish. Get the Hollandaise perfect and this earns its place every time.
Elevated — Honey waffle, black pudding, Hollandaise (Fallow-inspired)
Swap English muffin for a house honey waffle. Swap bacon for Stornoway black pudding. Immediately more interesting. Still totally accessible. The sweet-savoury base does the work.
On Trend — Za'atar crumpet, spiced chicken, sumac Hollandaise (Imad's-inspired)
House crumpet toasted in za'atar butter. Crispy spiced chicken thigh. Classic Hollandaise spiked with sumac and a pinch of cumin. Familiar architecture, completely fresh flavours. Social-media-ready.
Boundary Push — House sourdough muffin, 48hr braised beef short rib, truffle Hollandaise
Your "signature" Benedict. Braised beef from the kitchen's own stock. Truffle oil in the Hollandaise. House-baked muffin. The one dish that tells the whole story of what you're trying to do — in a single plate.
Menu Application

Run two Benedicts on the menu at the same time: the Classic for the crowd-pleasers and one "House Benedict" that rotates monthly with the best seasonal flavour combination. Keep the Hollandaise consistent across both — the base is the skill signal. The rotating special gives regulars a reason to come back each month and gives you a social content hook to post about. Your market-town audience will appreciate the classic; your adventurous regulars will come for the special.

6 ideas Friday & Saturday nights — a focused pizza operation with a clear point of view, seasonal topping rotations, and one trending format to try
Bon Appétit Pizza
Timeless Both

Una Pizza Napoletana

Lower East Side, New York City

Anthony Mangieri makes four or five pizzas a day, maximum, from 48–72-hour cold-fermented dough. That's it. No sides, no apps, no desserts except a daily sorbetto. The discipline is the product. Best Neapolitan pizza in the US — arguably the world. The philosophy matters as much as the recipe.

The Four Pizzas
Marinara — No Cheese
Crushed San Marzano DOP tomato, dried oregano, garlic, olive oil. No cheese. The oldest pizza in Naples — and the truest test of your dough and your tomato. If this is good, everything else is good.
Margherita — The Standard
San Marzano, fior di latte, fresh basil, olive oil. Every decision here — ferment time, water temperature, salt percentage, bake temperature — is non-negotiable. The world's most-ordered pizza done at its absolute best.
Cosacca — Cheese Without Mozzarella
San Marzano, aged pecorino romano, fresh basil, no mozzarella. Saltier, sharper, more complex. A pizza for people who already love pizza.
48–72hr Cold Fermented Dough
Cold-proving the dough for up to 72 hours develops complex flavour, creates the leopard-spot char on the crust, and makes the dough simultaneously chewy and digestible. This is the single most important decision in making great pizza.
Menu Application

The lesson from Una is discipline: do fewer things better. If you're running pizza two nights a week, you don't need eight pizzas — you need four or five that are genuinely outstanding. Start by mastering your dough ferment time. Cold-prove for at least 48 hours if you're not already. Then put a Marinara on the menu — no cheese, just tomato, garlic, oregano, olive oil. If people order it and love it, your pizza operation has arrived.

Honest Crust

Altrincham Market, Greater Manchester

Named in the Good Food Guide 2025 as one of the UK's best pizza operations. Started as a market stall, now a permanent fixture. Sourdough base, wood-fired, seasonal British toppings. The UK version of the Neapolitan philosophy — and the most relevant comparator for a market-town operator in the UK.

Key Dishes
Asparagus, Burrata & Prosciutto
Seasonal British asparagus spears, torn burrata added post-bake, prosciutto di Parma, lemon zest, good olive oil. White base (fior di latte only). The seasonal pizza that earns the price point and tells the story of the year.
Wild Garlic Oil Bread
Pizza dough flattened and baked with house wild garlic oil, sea salt, and optional aged parmesan. The table bread that makes everyone order another. Seasonal — only available when wild garlic is running (March–May).
Leopard-Spot Sourdough Base
The hallmark of a properly fermented and properly baked pizza — black blistered spots on the crust from high heat and good sugar development in the dough. If you can achieve the leopard spot consistently, the crust sells itself.
Menu Application

Honest Crust's model is the closest to yours in the UK — market town, sourdough, seasonal, pizza as the evening centrepiece. The asparagus/burrata/prosciutto white pizza is your summer star. Run it in June and July while British asparagus is at its best, then rotate to a courgette flower or sweetcorn version in August. The wild garlic bread is worth noting for spring, but any herb oil flatbread (basil, rosemary, chive) works as a year-round table-bread upgrade that earns its spot on the menu.

'Nduja + Hot Honey

Pizza Trend · 2025 · UK & US

The defining pizza flavour combination of 2024–2025. 'Nduja (spreadable, intensely spiced Calabrian cured pork) as a sauce base or topping, finished post-bake with a drizzle of hot honey. Simultaneously spicy, rich, sweet and floral. Now appearing on mainstream UK pizza menus — but still under-represented outside of cities.

The Trend in Detail
'Nduja as Base Sauce
Spread directly onto stretched dough instead of (or in addition to) tomato. 'Nduja melts and caramelises in the oven, creating a spiced red base with pockets of intense porky flavour throughout the cheese.
Hot Honey Drizzle — Post Bake
Chilli-infused honey drizzled over a hot pizza straight from the oven. The heat of the pizza activates the chilli further; the honey cools into a sticky glaze. Always added after baking — heat destroys the floral honey notes.
Full Build: 'Nduja, Fior di Latte, Hot Honey, Ashmore Cheddar
Ashmore or Montgomery cheddar adds a British-aged note that balances the 'nduja's heat. A UK-inflected version of the American hot honey pizza that has its own identity.
Menu Application

'Nduja is available from good suppliers (Brindisa, Natoora) and lasts well. Hot honey takes 30 minutes to make: warm honey, add dried chilli flakes, let steep, strain. Total cost: near zero. Run this as your house signature: 'Nduja, fior di latte, Ashmore cheddar, hot honey, torn basil. Name it something local. It will be your most-ordered pizza within a month. Do not overthink it — the combination is already perfect.

Mortadella & Stracciatella

Pizza Trend · Italian Fine-Casual · London 2025

The "cold-topped" pizza trend, popularised by London Italian restaurants and spreading to independent operators. The base bakes hot; cold, luxurious ingredients are applied immediately after — cold cream cheese, ribbons of cured meat, fresh herbs. The contrast between hot dough and cold topping is the whole point.

The Build
White Base — Fior di Latte Only
Simple fior di latte, olive oil, salt on the base. No tomato. This is the canvas — the cold toppings are the painting.
Post-Bake: Thick-Cut Mortadella
Lay large thin ribbons (or folded thick-cut rounds) of mortadella immediately after the pizza leaves the oven. The warmth wilts the meat without cooking it — it becomes silky and sweet.
Post-Bake: Cold Stracciatella Dolloped On Top
Spoon cold stracciatella (the milky, stringy interior of a burrata) over the mortadella in three or four generous pools. It melts slightly but keeps its fresh dairy character.
Finish: Pistachio Pesto & Lemon Zest
A tablespoon of pistachio pesto (pistachios, basil, olive oil, no parmesan) spooned over the top, with fine lemon zest grated across. Bright, nutty, fresh — cuts through the richness of the cheese and meat.
Menu Application

This is your premium Friday/Saturday special pizza — price it £2 higher than your standard range, name it on a chalkboard special, and explain the cold-top concept to staff so they can sell it confidently. Stracciatella is available from Natoora or La Fromagerie; if unavailable, tear open a burrata and use the interior. Mortadella is cheap and widely available. The pistachio pesto can batch-prep for the week and lives in the fridge.

Seasonal British Pizza Toppings

Seasonal Rotation Strategy · Summer 2025

Run one rotating "seasonal pizza of the month" that changes with the British growing calendar. It signals the whole ethos of the restaurant in one dish: local produce, changing menu, awareness of where food comes from. The best independent UK pizza operations use a seasonal special to drive repeat visits from regulars.

The Summer Rotation
June — British Asparagus, Burrata, Prosciutto
White base, fior di latte, whole British asparagus spears (ends trimmed, run lengthways across the pizza), prosciutto di Parma, torn burrata post-bake, lemon zest, extra virgin olive oil. The window for British asparagus is narrow — make it a "here while it lasts" special.
July — Courgette Flower, Stracciatella, Lemon
White base, fior di latte, whole courgette flowers laid flat (stuff with ricotta if budget allows), cold stracciatella post-bake, torn basil, lemon zest, chilli oil. Courgette flowers are the showpiece summer ingredient — people photograph them every time.
August — Sweetcorn, Burrata, Hot Honey, 'Nduja
Charred sweetcorn cut from the cob (or whole cob strips as "corn ribs" on the side), 'nduja base, fior di latte, torn burrata post-bake, hot honey drizzle, torn basil. August sweetcorn is extraordinary — sweet, smoky when charred, and pairs perfectly with 'nduja heat.
Menu Application

One seasonal pizza special per month gives you: a social content pillar ("our June pizza is here"), a reason for regulars to return, a differentiated menu that changes without the full overhaul, and a vehicle for buying the best seasonal British produce in small quantities. Photograph it, name it with the month and the hero ingredient ("The June" or "Asparagus Season"), and sell it until the produce runs out. Then move on.

Detroit-Style

Pizza Format · Growing Fast in UK · 2025

Thick, rectangular, focaccia-like pizza baked in an oiled blue steel pan until the cheese caramelises against the sides (the "frico crust"). Sauce goes on top, not underneath. Invented in Detroit in 1946; now experiencing a global revival. 2 in 5 UK consumers say they want to try it but haven't yet — the market is wide open outside London.

The Format
The Pan & The Dough
High-hydration dough pressed into an oiled rectangular blue steel pan. Prove in the pan for 2–4 hours. The oil fries the base and edges as it bakes, creating a crispy-bottomed, airy-centred pizza unlike any other style.
The Frico Crust — Caramelised Cheese Edges
Brick cheese (or a mix of mozzarella and aged cheddar) pressed right to the edges of the pan, not leaving a "crust rim." In the oven, the cheese melts against the hot pan sides and caramelises into a crispy, savoury cheese crust. This is the signature — and the reason people keep ordering it.
Sauce on Top (Wisconsin-Style)
Thick stripes of san Marzano sauce applied post-cheese, either before or after baking. The sauce sits on top rather than underneath — a deliberate reversal that keeps the base crispy and the sauce bright and fresh.
Menu Application

Detroit-style is an add-on format rather than a replacement — you don't need to change your existing pizza operation. A single blue steel pan (about £15) lets you test one Detroit pizza per service as a "format special." Given that 40% of UK pizza eaters want to try it but haven't, you'd be the first in your market town to offer it — and that gap is your advantage. Start with the simplest build: cheese, sauce, pepperoni. Once the format is right, build seasonal versions.

British Summer Produce Calendar

Reference Guide · June – August

The foundation beneath every seasonal decision. Knowing what's at peak quality each month — and building dishes around that — is what separates a reactive menu from an intentional one. When produce is at its best, it costs less, tastes better, and the dish writes itself.

Peak Produce by Month
June
British asparagus (end of season — buy now), peas & broad beans (first flush), elderflower (peak — cordial, syrup, fritters), Jersey Royal potatoes, strawberries (first), radishes, spring onions, new season garlic, watercress
July
Courgettes & courgette flowers, heritage tomatoes (early), sweetcorn (early), cucumber, runner beans, French beans, chanterelle mushrooms, gooseberries, raspberries, summer blackcurrants, British cheese peak (soft)
August
Sweetcorn (peak), heritage & heirloom tomatoes (peak), peaches & nectarines, figs (early), plums, blackberries, late courgette flowers, corn-on-the-cob, borlotti beans, early autumn squash, Victoria plums
Menu Application

Build your menu refresh around three "hero ingredients" per month — one for brunch, one for pizza, one for the table bread or a starter. In June: asparagus leads. In July: courgette flower. In August: sweetcorn and heritage tomatoes. You don't need to change the whole menu — changing one or two items per month with the season creates the perception of a constantly evolving, engaged kitchen without the overhead of a full rewrite.

Fallow — Waste-First Specials

St James's, London — Michelin Bib Gourmand

Fallow's entire menu exists as an answer to one question: what can we make with what we have? Every dish has a waste narrative — not as a marketing gimmick but as a genuine operational discipline that produces more interesting food at lower cost. Great British Chefs profiled their approach as one of the most influential in the current UK scene.

The Zero-Waste Playbook
Mushroom Parfait
Foraged and cultivated mushrooms blended with butter and cream into a silky, spreadable parfait. Works as a starter, a canapé on pizza bread, or a sauce component. A technique that turns a cheap ingredient into something luxurious.
Corn Ribs — The Cob Reinvented
Corn on the cob cut lengthways into four rib-shaped strips, deep-fried until they curl dramatically, tossed in beef-fat butter, smoked paprika, and lime. The whole cob used, zero waste, Instagram-ready, the best snack on any menu.
Chelsea Tart from Leftover Whey
The butter-making process produces gallons of whey — Fallow enriches Chelsea tart dough with it instead of discarding it. The result is a slightly tangy, complex pastry that has its own identity. Waste as flavour.
Guinness Soft-Serve
Leftover Guinness from the bar blended into an ice cream base and served through a soft-serve machine. The bitter-sweet, roasted flavour of stout in a soft-serve is unexpectedly brilliant — and the story writes the menu note.
Menu Application

You are already running a bar. You have spent Guinness, leftover bread, vegetable trimmings, whey from butter, and braising liquids that most kitchens throw away. Start with the corn ribs — available as a pizza side from August, requiring only corn, beef dripping (or chicken fat), and seasoning. Then think about your bar: what leftover spirits or beer could become a dessert component? The "waste story" turns a cheap process decision into a talking point on every table. This is where craft restaurants beat chains — chains never do this.

Osip, Bruton

Bruton, Somerset — Michelin Star

Merlin Labron-Johnson's farm-to-table restaurant in Bruton, Somerset — one of the most important restaurants in the UK right now. The menu is entirely dictated by the farm delivery that week. No menu is planned more than a few days out. Featured in Bon Appétit's Best New Restaurants list as a global reference point for produce-led cooking.

The Philosophy in Practice
Farm-Dictated Weekly Menu
The kitchen receives a farm delivery, then builds the menu from what arrived. This forces creative problem-solving, ensures peak-season quality on every plate, and creates a menu that can never be copied — because it won't exist next week.
Whole Animal + Root to Leaf
Every cut of meat, every vegetable trim, every bone, every herb stem finds its way onto a plate. The charcuterie board uses offcuts from the week's protein; the sauces are built from the bones; the salads use the outer leaves.
Preserved Summer for Winter Menus
In summer, Osip pickles, ferments and preserves at scale. Tomatoes preserved in July appear on the January menu. Elderflower cordial made in June becomes a dessert component in December. The seasonal menu extends well beyond the season itself.
Menu Application

You don't need to go full Osip — but the preserving practice is immediately applicable. When June elderflowers are free and abundant, make a batch of cordial and syrup for the bar and the dessert menu. When August tomatoes are at peak glut, roast and preserve a batch for October pizza sauce. When summer berries overflow, make compotes and jams that extend well beyond their season. This approach costs almost nothing extra and gives your winter menu access to summer's best flavours — at a fraction of the cost of buying them out of season.

Summer Sharing Starters

Antico Nuovo (SF) + Manteca (London) · Bon Appétit

Sharing tables built around bread, fat and fire — the two restaurants Bon Appétit used as the archetype for the best-value, highest-repeat summer dining experience. The shared principle: arrive with something hot on the table within five minutes, and the whole meal is set up for success.

Key Dishes
Hot Focaccia with Cultured Butter & Sea Salt
Fresh from the oven, rosemary-dimpled, generous with good olive oil. Cultured butter (tangier, more complex than regular) in a ramekin, Maldon sea salt. Arrives in under 3 minutes. The table is immediately happy. The bill is higher because people are immediately comfortable.
'Nduja Mussels with Grilled Bread
Fresh mussels steamed open in a 'nduja and white wine broth, finished with cream and parsley. Served with thick-cut grilled bread for scooping. Summer, spicy, dramatic — and done in under 8 minutes.
Duck Liver Parfait on Toast
Silky duck liver parfait, slightly sweet from a port reduction, on charred sourdough toast with cornichons and pickled shallot. The most profitable starter on any menu — cheap to make, premium to eat, completely seasonal-agnostic.
Hot Tomato Focaccia (August special)
Focaccia topped with halved heritage cherry tomatoes pressed into the dough, finishing with sea salt and good olive oil. August only — when British tomatoes are sweet enough to not need any sauce at all. The focaccia becomes the pizza and the side in one.
Menu Application

The focaccia-with-butter arrival is your highest-ROI menu change — it costs almost nothing to produce at scale, immediately elevates the dining experience, and sets the tone for the whole meal. If you are not already doing this, start now. The duck liver parfait is your most profitable starter and deserves permanent status. The 'nduja mussels are a perfect Friday/Saturday evening starter that moves product from the pizza prep (you already have 'nduja). Three dishes, all interconnected, all reinforcing the same kitchen philosophy.

Summer Dessert Strategy

Dessert Menu Framework · Summer 2025

The dessert menu is the last thing guests eat and the thing they talk about on the walk home. A summer dessert menu built around British seasonal fruit — strawberries, peaches, plums, blackberries — with one show-stopping centrepiece and two simpler crowd-pleasing options is all you need.

The Summer Dessert Roster
British Strawberry Tart with Clotted Cream Custard
Shortcrust pastry case, clotted cream custard filling (use actual clotted cream for the custard — it's incomparably rich), glazed British strawberries, optional elderflower jelly. The dessert of the British summer. Only worth making when British strawberries are at peak (June–July).
Peach Melba French Toast
Thick-cut brioche soaked overnight in vanilla custard, fried until bronzed and custardy, topped with grilled peach halves, raspberry sauce and a scoop of vanilla ice cream. A modern riff on an Escoffier classic — recognisable, nostalgic, and more interesting than it sounds. August hero.
Baked Alaska — The Show-Stopper
Sponge base, seasonal sorbet or ice cream centre (strawberry in June, raspberry in July, peach in August), encased in Italian meringue and torched at the table. The one dessert that makes the whole restaurant look up when it arrives. Reserve for weekend nights — it requires prep but produces maximum impact.
Sourdough Waffle, Stone Fruit Compote, Clotted Cream
House sourdough batter in a waffle iron, served with a warm plum or peach compote and a generous spoon of clotted cream. Simple, British, deeply satisfying. Works as a brunch dessert AND an evening one. Uses one waffle iron — the most versatile piece of equipment in the kitchen.
Menu Application

Three desserts is enough for a seasonal menu: one tart (changes monthly), one hot dessert (French toast or waffle with seasonal fruit), and one "event" dessert (Baked Alaska) that you flame at the table on weekends. The Baked Alaska is your biggest talking point — one meringue-and-torch drama per evening is worth more in word-of-mouth than five understated plated desserts. The sourdough waffle crosses the brunch/dinner divide, which saves equipment and prep. British strawberries in June, peaches in August — let the fruit calendar drive the menu, not the other way around.
