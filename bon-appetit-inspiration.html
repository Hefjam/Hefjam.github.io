Hide, Mayfair

Piccadilly, London — Michelin Star

Ollie Dabbous's nature-led restaurant. Their brunch translates fine-dining thinking into casual formats — the Benedict upgrade that London's been copying for two years.

Steal This Now

Whisk a teaspoon of truffle oil or anchovy paste into your next Hollandaise. One ingredient addition lifts the classic Benedict into a signature without changing any other skill.

Key Dishes
Lobster Benedict
Whole lobster claw and tail, poached egg, Hollandaise made with lobster butter on a brioche muffin. The ultimate Benedict upgrade — same architecture, completely different ambition.
Smoked Eel Omelette
Barely-set silky omelette folded over house-smoked eel, crème fraîche, chive. British fish, French technique — the combination that woke up a generation of brunch chefs.
Birch Sap Croissants
Laminated dough enriched with foraged birch sap instead of water. Served with cultured butter and seasonal honey. The ingredient narrative is the sell.
Menu Application

The "Benedict upgrade" framework — premium protein, premium base, upgraded sauce — is your single show-stopper plate. A braised short rib Benedict on a house-toasted sourdough muffin with truffle Hollandaise signals the whole operation in one dish. You don't need the lobster; you need the intention.

Fallow, St James's

St James's, London — Michelin Bib Gourmand

Jack Croft and Will Murray's waste-first restaurant. Their brunch menu is the most-copied by ambitious UK restaurants right now — zero-waste thinking meets genuinely fun food.

Steal This Now

Swap your English muffin for a house honey waffle on one Benedict this week. Source a local honey, make or buy a quality waffle batter, charge £1.50 more. The sweet-savoury contrast does everything.

Key Dishes
Black Pudding Benedict on Honey Waffle
Crispy Stornoway black pudding, poached egg and Hollandaise on a house honey waffle. The waffle is the genius move — sweet-savoury contrast, great texture, impossible to find anywhere else.
Hash Browns with Walnut Ketchup
Shredded potato cakes, perfectly crisp, with a house walnut ketchup made from fermented walnuts, vinegar and spice. Replaces tomato ketchup with something far more interesting.
Corn Ribs with Beef Butter
Corn cobs cut into rib strips, deep-fried until they curl, tossed in beef-fat butter with chilli. Zero-waste, Instagram-ready, addictive brunch side.
Chelsea Tart from Leftover Whey
Classic Chelsea tart dough enriched with the whey left from making butter. A pastry that turns a kitchen by-product into a talking point.
Menu Application

The honey waffle is immediately actionable this week. The corn ribs are your cheapest, most dramatic August side — buy whole cobs, cut into strips, fry, toss in dripping, charge starter price. The walnut ketchup could become your house condiment — one fermented sauce that signals the whole ethos of the kitchen.

Imad's Syrian Kitchen

Soho, London

Imad Alarnab's Syrian kitchen applies Middle Eastern flavour vocabulary to Western brunch formats. The result is the most talked-about Benedict riff in London right now.

Steal This Now

Toast your crumpets in za'atar butter (za'atar + unsalted butter, warm through). A crumpet + za'atar butter + a fried egg is already a dish. It's also the base of your next Benedict special.

Key Dishes
Shawarma Benedict
Za'atar-spiced crumpet base, crispy spiced chicken thigh, poached egg, Hollandaise spiked with sumac. The architecture of a Benedict; nothing tastes expected. Social-media-ready every service.
Ful Medames
Slow-cooked fava beans with olive oil, garlic, cumin and lemon, poached egg, crispy shallots. Levantine breakfast staple — hearty, healthy, vegan by default, excellent margin.
Knafeh French Toast
Thick brioche in orange blossom custard, fried until crunchy, topped with warm cheese, shredded kadaifi pastry and rose syrup. Syrian dessert meets French toast — the brunch showpiece.
Menu Application

The Shawarma Benedict is your monthly rotating special — run it for four weekends, photograph it once, post it. The ful medames is a cheap, vegan-solid, genuinely interesting main that lifts your plant-based offer without compromise. Both dishes cost almost nothing to prep and feel like they came from a restaurant that knows exactly what it's doing.

Roe, Canary Wharf

Canary Wharf, London — Fallow Sister Restaurant

The Fallow team's seafood-led sibling restaurant. Their pressed croissant sandwich format is the brunch move most-imitated across London right now.

Steal This Now

Use a panini press or heavy cast iron on a quality all-butter croissant filled with portobello mushroom and taleggio. If you don't have taleggio, use gruyère. Test it today — if it works, it's on the menu Monday.

Key Dishes
Croissant Royale — Crispy Portobello
Croissant pressed flat, filled with whole crispy portobello, melted taleggio, truffle aioli, pickled shallot. Hot and crisp outside, layered and molten within. The elevated toasted sandwich.
Croissant Royale — Salt Cod & Green Sauce
Salt cod, crispy potatoes, house verde (parsley, capers, anchovy, lemon) pressed in a croissant. A brunch that tastes like a Spanish coastal holiday.
House-Cured Salmon on Sourdough Rye
48-hour dry-cured salmon, crème fraîche, shaved pickled cucumber, dill oil. Clean, premium, repeatable.
Menu Application

Upgrading "toasted sandwich" to "pressed croissant" shifts the entire perception of your light-lunch offer at near-zero extra cost. The mushroom + taleggio filling is vegetarian, year-round, high-margin, and genuinely impressive. This is your lunch crossover dish — works 11am to 3pm without feeling out of place at either end.

Honey & Co

Bloomsbury, London — Israeli/Palestinian Kitchen

Sarit Packer and Itamar Srulovich's landmark Israeli kitchen, consistently named one of London's best brunch spots by Time Out. Their mezze-style sharing brunch proved a Middle Eastern breakfast format travels perfectly to UK all-day dining.

Steal This Now

Build one mezze breakfast board: house hummus, warm flatbread (pitta or tear-and-share sourdough), soft-boiled egg, za'atar, pickled cucumber, seasonal veg. Four components, one board, shareable, premium-priced.

Key Dishes
Big Breakfast Mezze Board
Shakshuka, creamy hummus, house labneh, warm burekas (filo pastry pockets filled with cheese or spinach), soft-boiled egg, seasonal pickle, fresh flatbread. The whole table sharing one abundant board — the opposite of the individual-plate brunch.
Sabich
Fried aubergine, hard-boiled egg, Israeli salad, tehina, amba (pickled mango sauce) in warm pitta. Tel Aviv street food that works perfectly as a brunch main — hearty, complex, naturally vegan-adaptable.
Creamy Hummus with Green Chilli & Egg
House-blended hummus (silkier and creamier than anything bought), topped with whole soft-boiled egg, green chilli, olive oil, warm flatbread. A brunch main for under £12 that feels like a full experience.
Burekas
Crisp-edged filo parcels filled with cheese (feta + ricotta) or spinach and cheese. Served warm with a small pot of yoghurt. House-made, quick to bake off in a hot oven from prepped raw state.
Menu Application

The mezze brunch board is your most accessible crowd-pleaser addition: hummus, flatbread, egg, za'atar, pickles — all things you either already buy or can make in minutes. For a market-town crowd that knows shakshuka from supermarket recipe kits, your scratch version with proper spicing and house flatbread is transformative. The sharing format also increases covers-per-table time and average spend without adding complexity to service.

Martino's

Chelsea, London — Opened 2025

Named Time Out London's standout new Italian opening of 2025. A 1950s–60s Italian trattoria format running from breakfast through to late dinner — the all-day Italian model proving its appeal with a Millennial and Gen Z crowd who want quality, nostalgia and simplicity in the same room.

Steal This Now

Replace your standard pancake batter with ricotta pancakes: same method, same pan, completely different texture — pillowy, rich, slightly tangy. Make the blueberry compote from frozen berries in 10 minutes. Put it on this weekend.

Key Dishes
Ricotta Pancakes with Blueberry Compote
Light, pillowy pancakes made with fresh ricotta in the batter — richer and less sweet than standard buttermilk, served with warm blueberry compote and a dusting of icing sugar. The Italian breakfast pancake that feels luxurious without being heavy.
Arrabbiata Baked Eggs on Sourdough
Eggs baked in a spiced tomato arrabbiata sauce, served bubbling in a cast-iron dish with thick-cut toasted sourdough. A shakshuka cousin with an Italian accent — bold, comforting, simple to execute at scale.
Bircher Muesli with Apple, Dates & Hazelnuts
Oats soaked overnight in apple juice, grated apple stirred through, topped with chopped Medjool dates and toasted hazelnuts. Prep-heavy once, zero effort at service. The lighter brunch option done with intention.
House Cornetto Pastries
Italian-style horn pastry (less rich than French croissant, slightly sweeter), served warm with breakfast. A pastry that signals a different cultural reference and travels well alongside coffee.
Menu Application

The arrabbiata baked eggs is a direct alternative to shakshuka with an Italian angle — if you're leaning into a pizza-brunch identity, the Italian-inflected egg dishes are more coherent on the same menu. The ricotta pancake is a simple swap that gives your pancake stack a story and a texture difference worth paying for. Martino's success proves the Italian all-day model has mass UK appeal — and you're already most of the way there.

Kellogg's Diner

Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Est. 1928

A 100-year-old Brooklyn diner revived by chef Jackie Carnesi with Texas roots. Open 24/7 — the ultimate stress test of a brunch menu that actually works. Fine-dining precision applied to diner classics.

Steal This Now

Use your braised meat trimmings for a Saturday morning hash. Crisped short rib or pulled pork, diced potato, caramelised onion, fried egg on top. You're already doing the braise — this uses what would otherwise be kitchen waste.

Key Dishes
Guajillo Braised Short Rib Hash
Short rib slow-braised in guajillo chilli, shredded and crisped on a flat-top with diced potato and onion. The braising liquor deglazes the whole pan — every bite has depth. The best breakfast hash in New York.
Patty Melt on Rye
Beef patty, slow-griddled white onions and Swiss cheese pressed between marble rye, cooked flat until bronzed. Not a burger. Better than a burger. Absent from almost every British pub brunch menu — that's your gap.
Cornmeal Masa Pancakes
Gluten-free pancakes made with masa harina — buttery, subtly savoury, completely different texture to standard buttermilk pancakes. A pancake with a story.
Chilaquiles Verdes
Fried tortilla chips in bright tomatillo salsa, crema, cotija, fried eggs. Acidic, spicy, textural — the Tex-Mex brunch that's landed in London but is largely absent from market-town menus.
Menu Application

The braised meat hash closes the loop between your pizza-night kitchen and Saturday brunch service — the short rib braise you're already running for a pizza topping becomes your best brunch main the next morning at near-zero marginal cost. The patty melt is a genuinely underserved dish in British pubs — it's not a burger, it's not a sandwich, it's something better. Put it on lunch and watch what happens.

The Benedict Spectrum

Strategy Framework — Not a Restaurant

The Eggs Benedict is the most culturally elastic brunch format on any menu. The London scene in 2025 is defined by creative Benedict riffs. Here is the full spectrum from accessible to boundary-pushing — all using the same underlying technique.

Steal This Now

Put two Benedicts on the menu: a Classic for the crowd, and one rotating House Benedict that changes monthly. The rotating one gives regulars a reason to come back every month — and gives you a monthly social post that takes 30 seconds to write.

Low to High Boundary Push
Classic — English muffin, back bacon, Hollandaise
Your volume dish. Get the Hollandaise right — from scratch, every time — and this earns its place permanently. Consistency is the product.
Elevated — Honey waffle, black pudding, Hollandaise
Swap the muffin for a house honey waffle. Swap bacon for Stornoway black pudding. Accessible to anyone, interesting to everyone. The sweet-savoury base does the work.
On Trend — Za'atar crumpet, spiced chicken, sumac Hollandaise
House crumpet in za'atar butter, crispy spiced chicken thigh, Hollandaise spiked with sumac. Familiar architecture, completely fresh flavours. The social media special.
Boundary Push — Sourdough muffin, 48hr braised short rib, truffle Hollandaise
Your signature dish. House-baked muffin. Short rib from your own braise. Truffle oil in the Hollandaise. The one plate that tells the story of the whole kitchen in a single serving.
Menu Application

Keep the Hollandaise recipe identical across all variants — the base skill is the signal. Run the Classic as the permanent fixture and one rotating special as your "House Benedict of the Month." Your market-town regulars love the classic; your adventurous guests come for the special; your social media shows the journey. Three things from one menu decision.

Towpath Cafe

Canal-side, De Beauvoir, London — Time Out Top 10 Brunch

Lori de Mori and Laura Jackson's legendary open-kitchen cafe on the Regent's Canal towpath. No website, no reservations, no printed menu — dishes are written on a board each morning based on what arrived. The most-imitated brunch kitchen ethos in London: radical simplicity, impeccable sourcing, technique as the differentiator.

Steal This Now

Brown butter in a pan until nutty. Add a handful of sage leaves — they'll crisp in 30 seconds. Pour the whole thing hot over a fried egg. That's Towpath's most-stolen technique. It costs nothing extra, elevates any egg dish, and takes 90 extra seconds.

Key Dishes
Sliced Fried Eggs with Caramelised Sage & Chilli Butter
Eggs fried in good olive oil until edges are lacework-crispy, then sliced into ribbons. Separately, brown butter is pushed to the edge of burning, sage leaves crisped in 30 seconds, dried chilli stirred through, poured over the plate still sizzling. The technique is the dish — three ingredients, one process, extraordinary result.
Egyptian Eggs & Taramasalata
Eggs fried in a very hot, very generous pool of olive oil until edges blister and curl into lace — Egyptian-style. Served on a bed of house taramasalata made from actual smoked cod's roe (blitzed with good olive oil, lemon, soaked bread — never the pink packet). Warm flatbread alongside. A 10-minute dish that tastes like it took all day.
Fig Leaf Soft Serve
Fresh fig leaves steeped overnight in warm double cream, strained and churned. The leaves release a flavour somewhere between coconut, vanilla and warm stone — floral, entirely unexpected, nothing else tastes like it. Available July–September when leaves are on the tree. A dish no supplier can replicate for you.
Anchovy Butter Toast
Unsalted butter whipped with salted anchovy fillets until smooth, spread thick on charred sourdough. An aggressively simple dish that sells every single service. Batch-made and refrigerated for two weeks — set-up time is 5 minutes, service time is 2.
Menu Application

The Towpath philosophy is radical simplicity pushed to the point of complete confidence: three ingredients, one technique, executed perfectly every time. The sage brown butter egg costs the price of sage and 90 extra seconds. The anchovy butter toast is a table arrival or brunch starter that costs pennies and signals a kitchen that makes its own sauces. The fig leaf ice cream, when fig leaves are available later in summer, is the kind of dish no chain will ever make — yours by proximity to a garden or local farm. This is the gap you're operating in.

Rochelle Canteen

Arnold Circus, Shoreditch — Margot Henderson & Melanie Arnold

Hidden inside a converted bike shed in a Victorian school, Rochelle Canteen has operated quietly as one of London's most respected kitchens for 20 years. Margot Henderson's cooking is defined by absolute confidence in unfashionable British ingredients treated with proper technique. Great British Chefs profiled it as the template for the honest neighbourhood restaurant done right.

Steal This Now

Render your pork belly skin trimmings in a dry pan until they puff and blister into crackling. Season hard. Serve alongside any egg dish or spread as a textural element. Zero-waste, zero-cost, genuinely dramatic. This is the pig skin Rochelle serves with taramasalata — you can do this today.

Key Dishes
Smoked Cod's Roe & Crispy Pig Skin
House taramasalata from actual smoked cod's roe — blitzed with extra-virgin olive oil, lemon, soaked white bread, nothing artificial. Served with pork belly skin from butchery trim, rendered and fried until it puffs and blisters. The pig skin is the vessel that melba toast can never be: hot, salty, three-dimensional, made from kitchen waste. A brutally good combination that costs almost nothing.
Very Slow Scrambled Eggs with Anchovy Butter
15 minutes on the lowest heat, constant gentle stirring, pulled off while still molten. Finished with a pat of anchovy butter that melts silently into the eggs on the plate. The scramble that makes you question every scrambled egg you've made before — the texture is silk, the flavour is complete.
Devilled Crab on Toast
Brown crab meat, English mustard, Worcestershire, Tabasco, butter — devilled hot and served on thick sourdough toast. British coastal ingredient, Victorian technique, zero faff. A brunch main or starter that signals the kitchen knows what it's doing before the eggs even arrive.
Grilled Flatbread with Brown Butter & Herbs
Flatbread baked on a dry griddle until charred in spots, brushed immediately with brown butter, scattered with picked fresh herbs. The simplest bread course in London — and the one guests remember most clearly. Uses whatever herbs are overabundant in the kitchen that day.
Menu Application

Rochelle's lesson: unfashionable ingredients treated with respect are more interesting than fashionable ones treated carelessly. Smoked cod's roe is cheap, available from any good fishmonger, and most customers have only had the bright-pink supermarket version — your scratch taramasalata is transformative. The crispy pig skin is free if you're already buying pork belly, and it converts kitchen trim into a dish worth charging starter prices for. These are the dishes that create stories, repeat customers, and the kind of reputation that takes years to build and a lifetime to lose.

Brother Marcus

Multiple London Sites — Levantine-Mediterranean Brunch

Named one of London's best all-day brunch restaurants by Time Out for three consecutive years. Brother Marcus runs the Levantine-Mediterranean brunch model that has proven itself across four London sites — kefir marination, grape molasses, and Eastern Mediterranean ingredient vocabulary applied to recognisably brunch-shaped dishes. The format that makes regulars come back every week.

Steal This Now

Marinate your next chicken thigh batch in kefir overnight instead of buttermilk. Kefir's lactic acid is more aggressive — the tenderising is dramatic, the crust when fried is visibly, audibly better. Same cost, same process, completely different result. Switch tonight.

Key Dishes
Kefir-Fried Chicken on Potato Rosti with Fried Eggs
Boneless thighs marinated overnight in kefir, dredged in seasoned flour, fried until the crust is shattering and golden-brown. Served on a pressed, pan-fried potato rosti with two fried eggs and a drizzle of harissa honey. The chicken-and-waffle format reoriented toward the Middle East — more interesting flavours, better technique signal, easier to execute than a waffle iron.
Toasted Banana Bread with Caramelised Bananas & Grape Molasses
House-baked banana bread, thickly sliced and toasted until the cut face is deeply caramelised, topped with pan-fried bananas in brown butter and a drizzle of grape molasses (pekmez — dark, complex, significantly less sweet than regular molasses). A sweet brunch plate that justifies dessert prices without feeling like a dessert. The grape molasses is the unfamiliar ingredient people ask about.
Shakshuka with Labneh & Sourdough
Eggs poached in a long-cooked spiced tomato and pepper sauce, finished with a generous dollop of strained yoghurt (labneh) that melts slightly into the sauce, thick-cut sourdough alongside. The labneh completely changes the richness and temperature balance — it cools pockets of the dish and adds a dairy tang that crème fraîche can't replicate.
Halloumi & Watermelon Board — August Special
Thick halloumi slabs grilled hard until golden, served alongside cold watermelon, fresh mint, pomegranate molasses and a dusting of sumac. The summer brunch sharing starter that photographs perfectly and takes 6 minutes to prepare. Arrives at the table before the main dishes and sets the tone for the whole meal.
Menu Application

The kefir swap is a technique upgrade that costs exactly the same as buttermilk and produces dramatically better results — the kind of change that separates kitchen craft from kitchen habit. The banana bread plate is your sweet brunch anchor: house-baked means a genuine story, grape molasses is the ingredient people ask about, and the whole dish takes 4 minutes to plate at service. The watermelon and halloumi board is your August sharing starter — seasonal, shareable, zero specialist equipment, extraordinary margin. These three together give the menu a coherent Levantine accent that works alongside your Italian pizza identity without contradiction.

Babka French Toast

Brunch Trend — London / New York / Tel Aviv 2024–2025

The fastest-growing premium French toast format. Babka — the Jewish braided enriched bread twisted with chocolate or cinnamon filling — does something standard brioche can't: the swirled layers caramelise differently when fried, creating an extraordinary marbled exterior with bitter-sweet pockets throughout. Pioneered in New York Jewish delis, now appearing on London menus at Chet's and beyond. In a market town, you're almost certainly first.

Steal This Now

Source babka from any good deli or Jewish bakery. Cut 4cm thick, soak in custard (eggs, cream, vanilla, cinnamon) for 15 minutes, fry on medium-low in clarified butter until mahogany brown all over — 4–5 minutes per side. The chocolate swirls caramelise against the pan and do the rest. This is on the menu this weekend.

The Build
The Babka Advantage Over Brioche
Brioche is a uniform enriched dough. Babka is braided around a chocolate or cinnamon-sugar filling — the layers behave entirely differently when soaked in custard and fried. The filling caramelises against the hot pan, creating bitter-sweet crispy pockets throughout the exterior. It is a fundamentally different French toast, not just a brioche upgrade.
Chocolate Babka French Toast — Full Build
4cm-thick chocolate babka, soaked in vanilla and orange zest custard for 15 minutes. Fried in clarified butter on low-medium heat for 4–5 minutes per side until mahogany brown and puffed. Served with Devon clotted cream, seasonal berry compote (British strawberries and raspberries right now), and a finish of flaked sea salt. The salt on chocolate is the move that separates this from every café version.
Cinnamon Babka — Autumn Pivot
Cinnamon-swirl babka in a cardamom custard, fried, served with warm spiced apple compote and crème fraîche. The same technique, the same supplier, a different seasonal story from September onward. One process gives you two seasonal menus.
The Supplier Strategy
Buy babka — don't make it. Any good deli or Jewish bakery supplies it. Small MOQ, extraordinary margin on the finished dish, and a provenance story (sourced from [bakery name]) that adds value to the menu description. The story is: Jewish bakery × French technique × British seasonal fruit × your kitchen. That's entirely yours.
Menu Application

This is your premium French toast — a meaningful upgrade from brioche with a story worth telling. Price it £2 above your standard French toast. Source the babka rather than making it. The dish is unusual enough to photograph, familiar enough to order without explanation, and the seasonal fruit topping gives you a monthly variation. In a market town, you are almost certainly the first to put babka French toast on a brunch menu within 30 miles — and the first wins the reputation.

Dishoom

Multiple UK Cities — Bombay Café, Good Food Guide Perennial

Dishoom recreates the Irani cafes of Bombay — open from breakfast through midnight, the same menu running all day. Their brunch is the most-booked in the UK outside London, with queues before opening on weekends. The relevance: their dishes are recognisably brunch-shaped (eggs, beans, bread) but the spice vocabulary is entirely their own. Every market-town customer already has a point of reference for Dishoom — that's your advantage.

Steal This Now

Masala scrambled eggs: cook diced tomato, green chilli and fresh ginger in butter until softened (4 minutes). Add beaten eggs, cook slowly with cumin and turmeric. Finish with coriander. It's scrambled eggs with five extra ingredients that cost pennies and completely transform the dish. On the menu tomorrow morning.

Key Dishes
Kejriwal — Fried Eggs on Chilli Cheese Toast
Thick-cut white bread toasted, spread with a mixture of melted cheese and chopped green chillies, two fried eggs on top, cracked black pepper. Named after a Delhi politician. One of the cheapest dishes on the menu, one of the most-ordered. The concept: familiar (egg on toast) made completely distinctive through one additional element (chilli cheese base).
House Black Daal
Black lentils cooked overnight (literally — on the stove for 24 hours) until silky and almost spreadable, with tomato, cream, garam masala. Served with naan baked in a tandoor. A brunch side that's also a vegetarian main — hearty, deeply flavoured, deeply affordable to produce in bulk.
Bacon Naan Roll
House-baked naan, streaky bacon, cream cheese, house tomato-chilli chutney. The bridge dish: takes a classic British format (bacon roll) and recontextualises it through a different bread. If you bake flatbreads, a naan is the next step. If you already make naan or pitta, this is instant.
Bun Maska — Bread & Butter
A soft-baked bun, split and served with cultured butter and house-made jam. The Dishoom version of a breakfast pastry — simple, branded, recognisable, but different enough from everything else on the table to be notable. Their cultured butter is the steal: more complex than supermarket butter, costs the same to buy from any good dairy supplier.
Menu Application

Dishoom's genius is that the dishes are familiar in form (eggs, bread, beans) but unfamiliar in flavour — accessible enough for the market-town crowd who's cautious about "foreign food," interesting enough for regulars who want something new every visit. The masala scrambled eggs require no new equipment and no specialist sourcing — cumin, turmeric, green chilli and fresh coriander are available in every supermarket. The Kejriwal (chilli cheese toast + fried eggs) is your most accessible bold move: it's eggs on toast with one extra step that completely changes the experience.

E5 Bakehouse & Jolene

Hackney / London Bridge — Natural Sourdough Bakery Brunch

Two of London's most influential bakery-restaurants — E5 Bakehouse in Hackney and Jolene on London Bridge — have built their entire identities around natural leavening, heritage grains and the conviction that great bread IS the kitchen. Their brunch model: exceptional sourdough as the anchor, seasonal veg and simple proteins around it. The bread is the star. Everything else is a vehicle for the bread.

Steal This Now

Toast your thick-cut sourdough cut-face down on a screaming hot dry pan or griddle until genuinely dark — past golden, into deep brown. The char is flavour, not a mistake. Serve with cultured butter and flaked sea salt. That's the best toast in the room. No extra cost. Just conviction about heat.

Key Dishes
Tartine — Open-Face Seasonal Toast
Thick sourdough charred on one side, cold butter spread on the hot surface so it half-melts, then topped with seasonal ingredients: ricotta + roasted figs + honey in autumn, smashed peas + mint + soft-boiled egg in spring, roasted heritage tomatoes + basil oil in August. The bread is the base; the seasonal topping is the menu rotation. One skill, endless variations.
Cultured Butter — The £0.50 Upgrade
Good-quality unsalted butter left at room temperature for 24–48 hours with a tablespoon of crème fraîche stirred through, then churned or worked with a wooden spoon until slightly tangy and spreadable. The fermentation from the crème fraîche changes the flavour profile entirely. Costs the same as regular butter to make. Worth double on the table.
Heritage Grain Porridge with Seasonal Fruit
Emmer, einkorn or spelt grain (not oats) slow-cooked with whole milk until thick and nutty, topped with warm seasonal fruit compote and honey. Farringdon Road, London

On Farringdon Road since 1869 — the original "progressive working class caterer" sign is still above the door. Steal This Now

Confit potatoes. Wafer-thin mandoline-sliced potatoes, layered in duck fat, cooked at 120°C until translucent, pressed overnight under weight, cut into blocks, deep-fried at 190°C for 4 minutes until croissant-gold. The exterior shatters. The interior is warm potato cream. On the menu since 2013, never removed. This is the brunch side dish that ends all arguments. Key Dishes
Confit Potatoes — Full Technique
Mandoline-slice potatoes wafer-thin. Toss in duck fat and salt. Layer one slice at a time in a lined terrine mould (or deep baking dish). Cook at 120°C for 1.5–2 hours until soft and translucent throughout. Press overnight in the fridge under weight — this compresses the layers into a dense, cuttable block. Cut into portions (rectangles or squares). Deep-fry at 190°C for 4 minutes until golden — the exterior shatters like glass, the interior is potato cream. Finish with Maldon salt. Serve immediately. The press overnight is non-negotiable: it is what enables clean cuts and the dramatic texture contrast.
Chicken Liver Parfait with Charred Sourdough
Livers cleaned of all sinew and membrane, blended with butter, eggs, cream, madeira, seasoned and strained through a fine sieve. Poured into a mould and baked in a bain-marie at 130°C until just set — it should still tremble in the centre when you remove it. Chilled under light pressure for a minimum of 6 hours. Served thin-sliced on charred sourdough with something sharp alongside: cornichons, pickled walnut, or a small amount of chutney. This is a legitimate brunch dish in France and should be on a serious British brunch menu. A 500g parfait serves 10–12 brunch portions.
Mangalitza Shoulder Croquettes with Pickled Walnut Ketchup
Mangalitza is the highest-fat heritage breed pig — almost a lard pig. Shoulder braised until falling-apart, pulled and mixed with a little of the braising liquor, pressed cold, cut into cylinders, crumbed in panko, fried until deeply golden. Pickled walnut ketchup: pickled walnuts (available from any good British deli or online) reduced and blended into a sauce. This is the "familiar format, unfamiliar quality level" move — a croquette everyone understands, but the quality of the breed and the British condiment make it something worth ordering again. A Friday pizza night starter or weekend brunch special.
Your Version — The Confit Everything Principle
The Quality Chop House operating principle generalises: any protein or vegetable confit in fat at low temperature, then finished at high heat, achieves a texture and flavour impossible any other way. Confit chicken legs (duck fat, thyme, garlic, 120°C, 2 hours) — crisp skin, falling-off-bone meat, brunch main. Confit garlic cloves (olive oil, low oven, 1 hour) — spreadable, sweet, zero sharpness — a house condiment. Confit cherry tomatoes (olive oil, basil, 90°C, 2 hours) — concentrated, jammy, versatile. The technique costs almost nothing in energy once you understand the principle. The result reads as restaurant-quality to any guest.

The confit potato is your first task. Buy a mandoline if you don't have one (£20, Kuhn Rikon or OXO), source a tin of duck fat (£4, any supermarket), and run the first batch this week. It takes 15 minutes of active time across two days. The result is the best potato you've ever served — and you will never serve a different potato again. The croquette is your Friday pizza night starter — braise a shoulder on Thursday, croquette on Friday. Pickled walnuts are on Amazon. The QCH's most important lesson: a technically perfect dish that never needs changing is worth more than ten interesting dishes that rotate. Find your confit potato. Keep it forever.

Padron peppers. Blister them in the hottest pan you have, finish with good flaked sea salt and a squeeze of lemon. The entire dish costs under £2 and takes 4 minutes.
