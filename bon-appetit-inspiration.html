Hide, Mayfair

Piccadilly, London — Michelin Star

Ollie Dabbous's nature-led restaurant. Their brunch translates fine-dining thinking into casual formats — the Benedict upgrade that London's been copying for two years.

Steal This Now

Whisk a teaspoon of truffle oil or anchovy paste into your next Hollandaise. One ingredient addition lifts the classic Benedict into a signature without changing any other skill.

Key Dishes
Lobster Benedict
Whole lobster claw and tail, poached egg, Hollandaise made with lobster butter on a brioche muffin. The ultimate Benedict upgrade — same architecture, completely different ambition.
Smoked Eel Omelette
Barely-set silky omelette folded over house-smoked eel, crème fraîche, chive. British fish, French technique — the combination that woke up a generation of brunch chefs.
Birch Sap Croissants
Laminated dough enriched with foraged birch sap instead of water. Served with cultured butter and seasonal honey. The ingredient narrative is the sell.
Menu Application

The "Benedict upgrade" framework — premium protein, premium base, upgraded sauce — is your single show-stopper plate. A braised short rib Benedict on a house-toasted sourdough muffin with truffle Hollandaise signals the whole operation in one dish. You don't need the lobster; you need the intention.

Fallow, St James's

St James's, London — Michelin Bib Gourmand

Jack Croft and Will Murray's waste-first restaurant. Their brunch menu is the most-copied by ambitious UK restaurants right now — zero-waste thinking meets genuinely fun food.

Steal This Now

Swap your English muffin for a house honey waffle on one Benedict this week. Source a local honey, make or buy a quality waffle batter, charge £1.50 more. The sweet-savoury contrast does everything.

Key Dishes
Black Pudding Benedict on Honey Waffle
Crispy Stornoway black pudding, poached egg and Hollandaise on a house honey waffle. The waffle is the genius move — sweet-savoury contrast, great texture, impossible to find anywhere else.
Hash Browns with Walnut Ketchup
Shredded potato cakes, perfectly crisp, with a house walnut ketchup made from fermented walnuts, vinegar and spice. Replaces tomato ketchup with something far more interesting.
Corn Ribs with Beef Butter
Corn cobs cut into rib strips, deep-fried until they curl, tossed in beef-fat butter with chilli. Zero-waste, Instagram-ready, addictive brunch side.
Chelsea Tart from Leftover Whey
Classic Chelsea tart dough enriched with the whey left from making butter. A pastry that turns a kitchen by-product into a talking point.
Menu Application

The honey waffle is immediately actionable this week. The corn ribs are your cheapest, most dramatic August side — buy whole cobs, cut into strips, fry, toss in dripping, charge starter price. The walnut ketchup could become your house condiment — one fermented sauce that signals the whole ethos of the kitchen.

Imad's Syrian Kitchen

Soho, London

Imad Alarnab's Syrian kitchen applies Middle Eastern flavour vocabulary to Western brunch formats. The result is the most talked-about Benedict riff in London right now.

Steal This Now

Toast your crumpets in za'atar butter (za'atar + unsalted butter, warm through). A crumpet + za'atar butter + a fried egg is already a dish. It's also the base of your next Benedict special.

Key Dishes
Shawarma Benedict
Za'atar-spiced crumpet base, crispy spiced chicken thigh, poached egg, Hollandaise spiked with sumac. The architecture of a Benedict; nothing tastes expected. Social-media-ready every service.
Ful Medames
Slow-cooked fava beans with olive oil, garlic, cumin and lemon, poached egg, crispy shallots. Levantine breakfast staple — hearty, healthy, vegan by default, excellent margin.
Knafeh French Toast
Thick brioche in orange blossom custard, fried until crunchy, topped with warm cheese, shredded kadaifi pastry and rose syrup. Syrian dessert meets French toast — the brunch showpiece.
Menu Application

The Shawarma Benedict is your monthly rotating special — run it for four weekends, photograph it once, post it. The ful medames is a cheap, vegan-solid, genuinely interesting main that lifts your plant-based offer without compromise. Both dishes cost almost nothing to prep and feel like they came from a restaurant that knows exactly what it's doing.

Roe, Canary Wharf

Canary Wharf, London — Fallow Sister Restaurant

The Fallow team's seafood-led sibling restaurant. Their pressed croissant sandwich format is the brunch move most-imitated across London right now.

Steal This Now

Use a panini press or heavy cast iron on a quality all-butter croissant filled with portobello mushroom and taleggio. If you don't have taleggio, use gruyère. Test it today — if it works, it's on the menu Monday.

Key Dishes
Croissant Royale — Crispy Portobello
Croissant pressed flat, filled with whole crispy portobello, melted taleggio, truffle aioli, pickled shallot. Hot and crisp outside, layered and molten within. The elevated toasted sandwich.
Croissant Royale — Salt Cod & Green Sauce
Salt cod, crispy potatoes, house verde (parsley, capers, anchovy, lemon) pressed in a croissant. A brunch that tastes like a Spanish coastal holiday.
House-Cured Salmon on Sourdough Rye
48-hour dry-cured salmon, crème fraîche, shaved pickled cucumber, dill oil. Clean, premium, repeatable.
Menu Application

Upgrading "toasted sandwich" to "pressed croissant" shifts the entire perception of your light-lunch offer at near-zero extra cost. The mushroom + taleggio filling is vegetarian, year-round, high-margin, and genuinely impressive. This is your lunch crossover dish — works 11am to 3pm without feeling out of place at either end.

Honey & Co

Bloomsbury, London — Israeli/Palestinian Kitchen

Sarit Packer and Itamar Srulovich's landmark Israeli kitchen, consistently named one of London's best brunch spots by Time Out. Their mezze-style sharing brunch proved a Middle Eastern breakfast format travels perfectly to UK all-day dining.

Steal This Now

Build one mezze breakfast board: house hummus, warm flatbread (pitta or tear-and-share sourdough), soft-boiled egg, za'atar, pickled cucumber, seasonal veg. Four components, one board, shareable, premium-priced.

Key Dishes
Big Breakfast Mezze Board
Shakshuka, creamy hummus, house labneh, warm burekas (filo pastry pockets filled with cheese or spinach), soft-boiled egg, seasonal pickle, fresh flatbread. The whole table sharing one abundant board — the opposite of the individual-plate brunch.
Sabich
Fried aubergine, hard-boiled egg, Israeli salad, tehina, amba (pickled mango sauce) in warm pitta. Tel Aviv street food that works perfectly as a brunch main — hearty, complex, naturally vegan-adaptable.
Creamy Hummus with Green Chilli & Egg
House-blended hummus (silkier and creamier than anything bought), topped with whole soft-boiled egg, green chilli, olive oil, warm flatbread. A brunch main for under £12 that feels like a full experience.
Burekas
Crisp-edged filo parcels filled with cheese (feta + ricotta) or spinach and cheese. Served warm with a small pot of yoghurt. House-made, quick to bake off in a hot oven from prepped raw state.
Menu Application

The mezze brunch board is your most accessible crowd-pleaser addition: hummus, flatbread, egg, za'atar, pickles — all things you either already buy or can make in minutes. For a market-town crowd that knows shakshuka from supermarket recipe kits, your scratch version with proper spicing and house flatbread is transformative. The sharing format also increases covers-per-table time and average spend without adding complexity to service.

Martino's

Chelsea, London — Opened 2025

Named Time Out London's standout new Italian opening of 2025. A 1950s–60s Italian trattoria format running from breakfast through to late dinner — the all-day Italian model proving its appeal with a Millennial and Gen Z crowd who want quality, nostalgia and simplicity in the same room.

Steal This Now

Replace your standard pancake batter with ricotta pancakes: same method, same pan, completely different texture — pillowy, rich, slightly tangy. Make the blueberry compote from frozen berries in 10 minutes. Put it on this weekend.

Key Dishes
Ricotta Pancakes with Blueberry Compote
Light, pillowy pancakes made with fresh ricotta in the batter — richer and less sweet than standard buttermilk, served with warm blueberry compote and a dusting of icing sugar. The Italian breakfast pancake that feels luxurious without being heavy.
Arrabbiata Baked Eggs on Sourdough
Eggs baked in a spiced tomato arrabbiata sauce, served bubbling in a cast-iron dish with thick-cut toasted sourdough. A shakshuka cousin with an Italian accent — bold, comforting, simple to execute at scale.
Bircher Muesli with Apple, Dates & Hazelnuts
Oats soaked overnight in apple juice, grated apple stirred through, topped with chopped Medjool dates and toasted hazelnuts. Prep-heavy once, zero effort at service. The lighter brunch option done with intention.
House Cornetto Pastries
Italian-style horn pastry (less rich than French croissant, slightly sweeter), served warm with breakfast. A pastry that signals a different cultural reference and travels well alongside coffee.
Menu Application

The arrabbiata baked eggs is a direct alternative to shakshuka with an Italian angle — if you're leaning into a pizza-brunch identity, the Italian-inflected egg dishes are more coherent on the same menu. The ricotta pancake is a simple swap that gives your pancake stack a story and a texture difference worth paying for. Martino's success proves the Italian all-day model has mass UK appeal — and you're already most of the way there.

Kellogg's Diner

Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Est. 1928

A 100-year-old Brooklyn diner revived by chef Jackie Carnesi with Texas roots. Open 24/7 — the ultimate stress test of a brunch menu that actually works. Fine-dining precision applied to diner classics.

Steal This Now

Use your braised meat trimmings for a Saturday morning hash. Crisped short rib or pulled pork, diced potato, caramelised onion, fried egg on top. You're already doing the braise — this uses what would otherwise be kitchen waste.

Key Dishes
Guajillo Braised Short Rib Hash
Short rib slow-braised in guajillo chilli, shredded and crisped on a flat-top with diced potato and onion. The braising liquor deglazes the whole pan — every bite has depth. The best breakfast hash in New York.
Patty Melt on Rye
Beef patty, slow-griddled white onions and Swiss cheese pressed between marble rye, cooked flat until bronzed. Not a burger. Better than a burger. Absent from almost every British pub brunch menu — that's your gap.
Cornmeal Masa Pancakes
Gluten-free pancakes made with masa harina — buttery, subtly savoury, completely different texture to standard buttermilk pancakes. A pancake with a story.
Chilaquiles Verdes
Fried tortilla chips in bright tomatillo salsa, crema, cotija, fried eggs. Acidic, spicy, textural — the Tex-Mex brunch that's landed in London but is largely absent from market-town menus.
Menu Application

The braised meat hash closes the loop between your pizza-night kitchen and Saturday brunch service — the short rib braise you're already running for a pizza topping becomes your best brunch main the next morning at near-zero marginal cost. The patty melt is a genuinely underserved dish in British pubs — it's not a burger, it's not a sandwich, it's something better. Put it on lunch and watch what happens.

The Benedict Spectrum

Strategy Framework — Not a Restaurant

The Eggs Benedict is the most culturally elastic brunch format on any menu. The London scene in 2025 is defined by creative Benedict riffs. Here is the full spectrum from accessible to boundary-pushing — all using the same underlying technique.

Steal This Now

Put two Benedicts on the menu: a Classic for the crowd, and one rotating House Benedict that changes monthly. The rotating one gives regulars a reason to come back every month — and gives you a monthly social post that takes 30 seconds to write.

Low to High Boundary Push
Classic — English muffin, back bacon, Hollandaise
Your volume dish. Get the Hollandaise right — from scratch, every time — and this earns its place permanently. Consistency is the product.
Elevated — Honey waffle, black pudding, Hollandaise
Swap the muffin for a house honey waffle. Swap bacon for Stornoway black pudding. Accessible to anyone, interesting to everyone. The sweet-savoury base does the work.
On Trend — Za'atar crumpet, spiced chicken, sumac Hollandaise
House crumpet in za'atar butter, crispy spiced chicken thigh, Hollandaise spiked with sumac. Familiar architecture, completely fresh flavours. The social media special.
Boundary Push — Sourdough muffin, 48hr braised short rib, truffle Hollandaise
Your signature dish. House-baked muffin. Short rib from your own braise. Truffle oil in the Hollandaise. The one plate that tells the story of the whole kitchen in a single serving.
Menu Application

Keep the Hollandaise recipe identical across all variants — the base skill is the signal. Run the Classic as the permanent fixture and one rotating special as your "House Benedict of the Month." Your market-town regulars love the classic; your adventurous guests come for the special; your social media shows the journey. Three things from one menu decision.

Una Pizza Napoletana

Lower East Side, New York City

Anthony Mangieri makes four or five pizzas a day from 48–72hr cold-fermented dough. No sides, no apps. The discipline is the product — and the lesson every pizza operator needs to hear.

Steal This Now

Put a Marinara on the menu this Friday — no cheese, just great San Marzano tomato, garlic, dried oregano, olive oil. If it sells and people love it, your dough and your tomato are good enough to build on.

The Pizzas
Marinara — No Cheese
Crushed San Marzano DOP, dried oregano, garlic, olive oil. No cheese. The oldest pizza in Naples and the truest test of dough quality and tomato quality. If this is good, everything is good.
Margherita — The Standard
San Marzano, fior di latte, fresh basil, olive oil. Every technical decision — ferment time, water temperature, bake temperature — is non-negotiable. The world's most-ordered pizza at its absolute best.
Cosacca — Without Mozzarella
San Marzano, aged pecorino romano, fresh basil, no mozzarella. Saltier, sharper, more complex. A pizza for people who already love pizza.
48–72hr Cold Fermented Dough
Cold-proving for up to 72 hours develops complex flavour, creates the leopard-spot char, and makes the dough simultaneously chewy and digestible. The single most important process decision in great pizza.
Menu Application

The lesson from Una is discipline: four or five outstanding pizzas beats eight average ones every time. If you're running pizza two nights a week, the constraint is actually your advantage — fewer choices, higher execution. Start with extending your cold ferment to 48+ hours if you're not already there. The Marinara is the benchmark — when it's excellent, add the seasonal specials on top of a solid foundation.

Asparagus, Burrata & Prosciutto

'Nduja + Hot Honey

Pizza Trend — 2024–2025 — UK & US

The defining pizza combination of the past two years. 'Nduja (spreadable Calabrian cured pork) plus post-bake hot honey — simultaneously spicy, rich, sweet and floral. Mainstream in London; largely absent from market towns.

Steal This Now

Order 'nduja this week (Brindisa or any good deli supplier). Make hot honey tonight: warm honey, steep dried chilli flakes 20 minutes, strain. That's it. Run the pizza this Friday: 'nduja, fior di latte, hot honey post-bake, torn basil.

The Build
'Nduja as Base Sauce
Spread directly onto stretched dough in addition to (or instead of) tomato. 'Nduja melts and caramelises in the oven, creating a spiced red base with pockets of intense porky flavour throughout the cheese.
Hot Honey — Always Post-Bake
Drizzled over a hot pizza straight from the oven. Heat activates the chilli further; the honey cools into a sticky glaze. Never before baking — it burns and loses its floral notes.
Full Build: 'Nduja, Fior di Latte, Ashmore Cheddar, Hot Honey
Ashmore or Montgomery cheddar adds a British-aged note that balances the 'nduja heat. A UK-inflected version with its own identity — not just copying the American trend.
Menu Application

Total cost to make hot honey: pennies, 20 minutes. Total procurement change: one 'nduja order. Run this as your house signature pizza: 'nduja, fior di latte, Ashmore cheddar, hot honey, torn basil. Name it something local — after the street, the taproom, the town. It will be your most-ordered pizza within a month. Do not overthink it. The combination is already perfect.

Mortadella & Stracciatella

Pizza Trend — Italian Fine-Casual — London 2025

The cold-topped pizza trend: the base bakes hot, luxurious cold ingredients are applied immediately after. The contrast between charred dough and cold cream cheese is the entire point. Now spreading from London independents to the wider UK scene.

Steal This Now

Make pistachio pesto tonight — pistachios, basil, olive oil, pinch of salt, 10 minutes. Add it to one white pizza this weekend with mortadella and a torn burrata (or just the interior of a burrata — that's stracciatella). Post the photo.

The Build
White Base — Fior di Latte Only
Simple fior di latte, olive oil, salt. No tomato. This is the canvas — the cold toppings are the painting.
Post-Bake: Thick-Cut Mortadella
Large thin ribbons laid on immediately after leaving the oven. The warmth wilts the meat without cooking it — it becomes silky and sweet.
Post-Bake: Cold Stracciatella
Spoon cold stracciatella (the interior of a burrata) in generous pools over the mortadella. It melts slightly but keeps its fresh dairy character. If unavailable, tear open a burrata.
Finish: Pistachio Pesto & Lemon Zest
A tablespoon of pistachio pesto spooned over, fine lemon zest grated across. Bright, nutty, fresh — cuts through the richness of the cheese and meat.
Menu Application

This is your premium Friday/Saturday special — price it £1.50–2 higher than your standard range, name it on a chalkboard special. Pistachio pesto batches and refrigerates for a week. Mortadella is cheap and widely available. The key is explaining the cold-top concept to staff so they can sell it confidently: "the contrast between the hot dough and cold cheese is the whole idea."

Detroit-Style

Pizza Format — Fastest Growing in UK 2025

Rectangular, thick, focaccia-like pizza baked in an oiled steel pan until the cheese caramelises against the sides (the "frico crust"). Sauce on top, not underneath. 2 in 5 UK consumers want to try it but haven't — the market is wide open outside London.

Steal This Now

Order one blue steel baking pan (around £15). Use high-hydration dough, press it in, cheese right to the edges, sauce on top, bake until caramelised. Test it on a Tuesday prep session this week — one pan, one test, zero risk.

The Format
The Pan & The Dough
High-hydration dough pressed into an oiled rectangular blue steel pan, proved in the pan 2–4 hours. The oil fries the base and edges as it bakes, creating a crispy-bottomed, airy-centred pizza unlike any other style.
The Frico Crust — Caramelised Cheese Edges
Brick cheese or mozzarella/aged cheddar mix pressed to the edges of the pan. In the oven, the cheese melts against the hot pan sides and caramelises into a crispy savoury crust. This is the signature — and the reason people keep ordering it.
Sauce on Top
Thick stripes of San Marzano sauce applied post-cheese, before or after baking. Sauce on top rather than underneath — keeps the base crispy and the sauce bright.
Menu Application

Detroit is an add-on format, not a replacement. A single blue steel pan lets you test one Detroit pizza per service as a weekly format special. You'd be the first in your market town to offer it — that gap is your advantage. Start with the simplest possible build: cheese, sauce, pepperoni. Once the format is right, build seasonal versions using the same topping rotation as your Neapolitan menu.

British Summer Produce Calendar

Reference Guide — June through August

The foundation beneath every seasonal decision. When produce is at peak quality it costs less, tastes better, and the dish writes itself. Three hero ingredients per month — one for brunch, one for pizza, one for a bread or starter — without a full menu rewrite.

Steal This Now

Order courgette flowers and broad beans from your veg supplier for this week. They move fast at peak season. Courgette flowers: pizza topping or brunch garnish. Broad beans: blitzed into a spread or left whole on a white pizza base.

Peak Produce by Month
June / Now
British asparagus (end of season — last chance), peas & broad beans (first flush, excellent), elderflower (peak — make cordial now), Jersey Royals, first strawberries, radishes, new-season garlic, watercress
July
Courgettes & courgette flowers, heritage tomatoes (early), sweetcorn (early), cucumber, runner beans, French beans, chanterelles, gooseberries, raspberries, blackcurrants
August
Sweetcorn (peak), heritage & heirloom tomatoes (peak), peaches & nectarines, figs (early), plums & Victoria plums, blackberries, late courgette flowers, borlotti beans
Menu Application

Pick three hero ingredients per month: one leads brunch, one leads pizza, one leads a bread or starter. June: asparagus. July: courgette flower. August: sweetcorn and heritage tomato. You don't need to rewrite the whole menu — changing two items per month with the season creates the perception of a constantly engaged kitchen at minimal overhead. When the ingredient is gone, say so. The scarcity is honest and it's compelling.

Fallow — Waste-First Specials

St James's, London — Michelin Bib Gourmand

Fallow's entire menu exists as the answer to "what can we make with what we have?" Every dish has a waste narrative — not as marketing but as genuine operational discipline. Great British Chefs profiled their approach as the most influential in the current UK scene.

Steal This Now

Save your corn cobs this August, cut lengthways into four strips, deep-fry until they curl, toss in beef dripping with smoked paprika. Corn ribs cost almost nothing and sell at full starter price. That's zero-waste thinking making you money.

The Zero-Waste Playbook
Mushroom Parfait
Forager's mushrooms blended with butter and cream into a silky spreadable parfait. Works as a starter, a canapé on pizza bread, or a sauce component. Turns a cheap ingredient into something luxurious through technique.
Corn Ribs — The Cob Reinvented
Corn on the cob cut lengthways into four rib strips, deep-fried until they curl dramatically, tossed in beef-fat butter, smoked paprika, lime. Zero waste, Instagram-ready, the best snack on any menu.
Chelsea Tart from Leftover Whey
Butter-making produces litres of whey — Fallow enriches Chelsea tart dough with it instead of discarding it. A slightly tangy, complex pastry that has its own identity. Waste as flavour.
Guinness Soft-Serve
Leftover Guinness from the bar blended into an ice cream base, served through a soft-serve machine. Bitter-sweet, roasted, unexpectedly brilliant — and the story writes the menu note for you.
Menu Application

You run a bar. You have spent Guinness, leftover bread, vegetable trimmings, whey from butter-making, braising liquids. The Guinness soft-serve is your low-hanging fruit — it's a talking point on every table and costs you almost nothing. The corn ribs are your August move. The "waste story" turns a cheap process decision into a competitive advantage that chains can never replicate, because they don't have your leftovers.

Brat, Shoreditch

Shoreditch, London — Michelin Star, Tomos Parry

Tomos Parry's Basque-inspired, fire-led restaurant named after the Welsh word for skate (the fish). The philosophy: find the best seasonal British ingredient, apply the right heat, then get out of the way. The most influential cooking philosophy for UK seasonal restaurants right now.

Steal This Now

Take one vegetable you're already ordering and char it directly on your grill or under your pizza oven broiler until genuinely blackened on the outside. Slice, dress with warm vinaigrette. Charred leeks, courgettes, spring onions — any of them. The char is the flavour. No extra seasoning required.

Key Dishes
Whole Turbot — Grilled in a Basket
A whole turbot grilled over lumpwood charcoal in a hand-made wire basket, basted with butter as it cooks. Tableside presentation, shared between two. The most dramatic sharing plate in London — the whole fish, the smoke, the ritual.
Leek Vinaigrette
Young leeks charred whole until blackened on the outside, then dressed warm with a house vinaigrette (sherry vinegar, Dijon, olive oil, capers). Costs pennies, looks and tastes extraordinary. The dish that made Brat famous on opening week.
Burnt Basque Cheesecake
Deeply caramelised exterior, molten cream cheese interior. No base, no fuss. Baked very hot, very briefly, until the top blackens deliberately. The dessert that proves restraint is a technique.

Menu Application

The leek vinaigrette is immediately actionable with zero procurement change — you're already ordering leeks. Char them whole in your pizza oven's residual heat before service, slice and dress. That's a seasonal starter or side at near-zero cost that signals exactly the right kitchen philosophy. The Basque cheesecake is your summer dessert centrepiece — bake it dark, serve it slightly warm, explain the deliberate char. It costs less than a tarte tatin and it converts every table to dessert orders.

St JOHN, Smithfield

Smithfield, London — Est. 1994, Fergus Henderson

Fergus Henderson's 30-year institution that essentially invented nose-to-tail eating as a philosophy. The menu changes twice daily, based on what's available. No ceremony, no garnish, no apology — just honest British cooking at its most confident.

Steal This Now

Put Welsh Rarebit on as a brunch side this week: aged cheddar, Worcestershire, English mustard, a splash of beer, on thick-cut toasted sourdough. Completely from your existing pantry. British, cheap, universally loved, and it signals a confident kitchen that isn't trying too hard.

Key Dishes & Principles
Bone Marrow with Parsley Salad on Toast
Roasted marrow bones, plain parsley and caper salad, thick-cut toast, sea salt. The St JOHN signature — zero technique flex, maximum flavour. A starter that costs almost nothing and becomes the thing people talk about for a week.
Devilled Kidneys on Toast
Lamb kidneys in a devilled sauce (Worcestershire, English mustard, Cayenne, cream), on thick toast. Classic British offal brunch — hearty, cheap, and the most powerful signal of a kitchen that's not afraid of anything.
Welsh Rarebit

Eccles Cake with Lancashire Cheese
Buttery flaky Eccles cake served warm alongside a sharp, crumbly Lancashire cheese. Sweet and savoury, British as it gets. A dessert or a cheese course — or both at the same time.
The Menu-Change Philosophy
The menu at St JOHN changes twice a day based on what's in season and what's arrived. One constant: a potato dish, a greens dish, and a roast. Everything else moves. The restraint of the fixed items makes the changing items feel more deliberate.
Menu Application

The St JOHN lesson isn't to put kidneys on your menu — it's to be confident enough to put something on the menu that not every customer will order, because the customers who do will never stop talking about it. One "brave" dish on every service — a devilled something, a rarebit, a bone marrow starter — is the thing that separates a pub restaurant from a restaurant that happens to be in a pub. Welsh rarebit is where you start: it's already in your kitchen, it costs almost nothing, and it's completely honest.

Osip, Bruton

Bruton, Somerset — Michelin Star, Merlin Labron-Johnson

The menu is entirely dictated by what the farm delivers that week. No advance planning beyond knowing what's growing. Featured in Bon Appétit's global best restaurants list as the reference point for produce-led cooking in the UK.

Steal This Now

Spend two hours this week making one batch preservation: elderflower cordial (in season right now), pickled radishes, or a roasted tomato conserva. That's one future menu ingredient created from what's cheapest right now, extending summer into October.

The Philosophy in Practice
Farm-Dictated Weekly Menu
The kitchen receives the farm delivery, then builds the menu from what arrived. Forces creative problem-solving, ensures peak-season quality on every plate, creates a menu that can never be copied because it won't exist next week.
Preserving Summer for Winter
In summer, Osip pickles, ferments and preserves at scale. Tomatoes preserved in July appear on the January menu. Elderflower cordial from June becomes a dessert component in December. The seasonal menu extends well beyond the season itself.
Whole Animal + Root to Leaf
Every cut of meat, every vegetable trim, every bone, every herb stem finds its way onto a plate. The charcuterie board uses offcuts; the sauces are built from bones; the salads use the outer leaves. Nothing is wasted.

Summer Dessert Strategy

Dessert Menu Framework — Summer 2025

The dessert is the last thing guests eat and the first thing they talk about on the walk home. Three desserts built around British seasonal fruit — one tart (rotating monthly), one hot dessert, one show-stopper — is all you need.

Steal This Now

Make a strawberry tart this week: clotted cream whisked with vanilla into a custard, topped with glazed British strawberries in a bought shortcrust case. June strawberries are extraordinary right now. This is your dessert of the month. It takes 30 minutes.

The Summer Dessert Roster
British Strawberry Tart with Clotted Cream Custard
Shortcrust case, clotted cream custard filling (use actual clotted cream — it's incomparably rich), glazed British strawberries. Optional elderflower jelly. June–July only — when British strawberries are at peak and the dish writes itself.
Burnt Basque Cheesecake
Deliberately blackened exterior, molten cream cheese interior, no base. Baked very hot and briefly. Looks dramatic, costs almost nothing, converts the whole table to dessert orders. Year-round anchor — doesn't need seasonal fruit.
Peach Melba French Toast — August
Thick-cut brioche soaked in vanilla custard, fried until bronzed, topped with grilled peach halves, raspberry sauce, vanilla ice cream. A modern Escoffier riff — recognisable, nostalgic, more interesting than it sounds. August is the window.
Sourdough Waffle with Stone Fruit Compote
Sourdough batter in a waffle iron, warm plum or peach compote, clotted cream. Works as brunch AND evening dessert from the same waffle iron — the most versatile piece of equipment in the kitchen. No dedicated pastry section needed.
Menu Application

Three desserts is the right number: one rotating tart (changes monthly with the season), one permanent show-stopper (Basque cheesecake — bake it dark, serve slightly warm, explain the deliberate char), one hot dessert (French toast or waffle with seasonal fruit). The Basque cheesecake crosses brunch and dinner with zero effort. The sourdough waffle uses one waffle iron and no dedicated pastry skill. The fruit tart changes monthly and gives you a social content hook every time. Start with the strawberry tart this week — the season is now.
